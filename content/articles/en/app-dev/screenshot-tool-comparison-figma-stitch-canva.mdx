## Introduction

Creating professional app store screenshots doesn't require mastery of one specific tool—the choice depends on your priorities. **Figma** excels at customization and design systems, **Canva** offers speed and pre-made templates, and **Stitch** combines AI-powered creativity with flexibility.

This article compares these three tools across six key dimensions—ease of use, template library, customization power, device frame quality, production speed, and output quality. We test each with the same realistic scenario and provide honest guidance so you choose wisely.

## Test Parameters

### Test App Scenario

We're creating screenshots for a "photo organization app" generated by Rork, featuring:
- Header with logo and search bar
- Grid-layout photo feed (3 columns)
- Bottom action buttons (share, delete, edit)
- Gradient background with subtle depth

### Output Specifications

- **App Store**: 1290 × 2796 px (6.7-inch)
- **Google Play**: 1080 × 1920 px (standard smartphone)
- **Languages**: Japanese and English
- **Quality**: PNG, high compression, 1-2 MB per file
- **Metrics**: Initial design time, revision time, language switching time

### Test Environment

- macOS 14.3, Chrome latest
- Figma Free plan, Canva Pro, Stitch (freemium)
- Real hands-on testing

## Detailed Tool Comparisons

### Figma — Maximum Creative Freedom, Steeper Learning Curve

**Ease of Use: 3.5/5**

Figma's power comes with complexity. Learning frames, layout grids, and plugins takes time, but once mastered, your design freedom is unmatched.

**Setup complexity:**
- Device mockups require plugin installation (Device Frame Generator)
- Gradient backgrounds take 5-6 steps in Design panel
- Text style consistency requires Assets panel management

**Template Library: 2.5/5**

Figma Community has templates, but app-screenshot-specific designs are limited (10-20 options). Expect to build most designs from scratch.

**Customization: 5/5**

Figma is king here. Colors, fonts, layouts, backgrounds—all fully adjustable. Rork-generated UIs can be refined with precision impossible in other tools.

**Customizable Elements:**
- Unlimited-color gradients
- Complete typography control (font, size, weight, line-height)
- Multi-layer shadows and effects
- Advanced masks and blend modes

**Device Frame Quality: 4.5/5**

Plugins like "Apple Devices" and "Device Mockups" produce realistic frames with excellent detail. Manual placement required for different device types, but realism is superior.

**Production Speed: 3/5**

- Initial setup: 15-20 minutes (plugins, grids)
- First screenshot: 40-60 minutes
- Second language: 5-10 minutes (text edits only)

After templating, subsequent versions are lightning-fast. Multi-version A/B testing becomes practical.

**Output Quality: 5/5**

PNG export quality is industry-leading. Compression settings allow perfect balance between file size and visual fidelity. SVG export also available.

**Best For:**
- Design system builders
- Multi-language projects with consistent templates
- Strong brand customization requirements
- Team-based design collaboration
- Frequent A/B testing needs

**Not Ideal For:**
- Speed-first mentality
- Avoiding complex settings
- Simple template-and-go workflows

### Canva — Fastest, Template-Rich

**Ease of Use: 4.5/5**

Canva's interface is beautifully simple. Drag-and-drop, search templates, customize instantly. Beginners grasp basics in 2-3 minutes.

**Simplicity Examples:**
- Template search: Find design in seconds
- Text editing: Double-click to edit
- Color changes: Select from palette, auto-apply

**Template Library: 5/5**

Hundreds of App Store and Google Play templates. Search "screenshot" and find immediately usable designs. Even without design sense, template selection yields professional results.

**Template Abundance:**
- App Store: 50+ templates
- Google Play: 50+ templates
- Style variations: Minimal, colorful, business, and more
- Industry-specific templates: Comprehensive

**Customization: 3/5**

Template customization is easy, but **fundamental restructuring is difficult**. Major background changes, layout recomposition, and complex effects are limiting.

**Limitations:**
- Gradients: Usually 2-color maximum
- Layout: Template grid structure is hard to override
- Fonts: Choose from pre-selected options only

**Device Frame Quality: 4/5**

Canva's device frames are polished. Latest iPhone and Android models supported, though older models have limited options. Quality is sufficient for store submission.

**Production Speed: 5/5**

- Template selection: 2-3 minutes
- Text edits: 3-5 minutes
- Color/style tweaks: 2-3 minutes
- **Total per language: 7-10 minutes**

Actual comparison:
- Figma: 60 minutes (first), 10 minutes (second language)
- Canva: 8 minutes (first), 5 minutes (second language)

**Output Quality: 4/5**

Quality is solid, though Figma edges ahead. Minor compression artifacts may appear on fine details and complex gradients, but most users won't notice.

**Best For:**
- Speed-first workflows
- Design newcomers
- Frequent multi-language updates
- Teams without design expertise
- Template-driven efficiency

**Not Ideal For:**
- Granular brand color matching
- Complex gradient effects
- Highly custom layouts

---

### Stitch — AI-Powered Creativity and Balance

**Ease of Use: 4/5**

Stitch features an AI assistant. Describe what you want ("photography app, blue tones, minimal style") and receive suggestions. Intuitive for creatives seeking guidance.

**AI-Assisted Workflow:**
1. Describe screenshot concept in text
2. AI generates multiple options
3. Choose one and refine details
4. Add/edit text
5. Export

**Smart Suggestions: 4/5**

App Store and Google Play templates are solid (30-40 options), plus AI can generate custom designs beyond templates.

**Customization: 4/5**

AI-generated designs can be refined significantly. More flexible than Canva, less powerful than Figma. High-level requests (e.g., "adjust colors to match app tone") work well.

**Customizable Elements:**
- AI-suggested color palettes (with tweaks)
- Limited typography control
- Template-based layout adjustments
- Re-generate with different tone/style

**Device Frame Quality: 4.5/5**

Near Figma-level quality. Latest devices supported, frames are realistic.

**Production Speed: 4/5**

- AI prompt input: 2-3 minutes
- Choose from suggestions: 1-2 minutes
- Text and fine-tuning: 5-10 minutes
- **Total: 10-15 minutes**

**Output Quality: 4.5/5**

High quality, minimal compression artifacts. Second only to Figma in visual fidelity.

**Best For:**
- Leveraging AI for creative ideas
- Balancing uniqueness and efficiency
- Designers wanting creative guidance
- Avoiding standard templates

**Not Ideal For:**
- Strict brand asset adherence
- Large template library preference

## Tool Selection Guide

### Choose Figma If You:

- Demand design customization
- Want to develop your design skills
- Build reusable templates for multiple projects
- Need precise Rork UI refinement
- Require strict brand consistency

**Ideal Scenarios:**
- Company design system integration
- Multi-project template library
- Monthly screenshot updates with high polish

### Choose Canva If You:

- Prioritize speed above all
- Have limited design experience
- Need frequent multi-language versions
- Love template library breadth
- Want minimum setup time

**Ideal Scenarios:**
- First app store launch
- Multiple language versions needed quickly
- Non-designer team creating screenshots

### Choose Stitch If You:

- Want creative AI suggestions alongside control
- Value uniqueness balanced with efficiency
- Have modest design experience but care about quality
- Enjoy generative design exploration

**Ideal Scenarios:**
- Competitive differentiation through unique designs
- AI-powered creative exploration
- Avoiding generic template appearance

## ASO Best Practices (Universal Across Tools)

Regardless of tool choice, follow these principles:

### 1. A/B Testing Framework

Create 2-3 screenshot variations and measure performance:

**Test Example:**
- Version A: Feature-focused ("5 Advanced Editing Features")
- Version B: Benefit-focused ("Make Your Memories Beautiful")
- Duration: 2-4 weeks
- Metric: Download rate, install rate

### 2. Compelling Headline Strategy

- **Slide 1**: Core benefit (emotion over features)
  - Example: "Turn Your Photos Into Art"
- **Slides 2-3**: Three specific features with action verbs
  - "AI Auto-Organize", "One-Tap Enhance", "Secure Cloud Save"
- **Slides 4-5**: Use cases ("Perfect for travel memories", "Family photo organization")

### 3. Visual Consistency

Unified background, fonts, and colors across all screenshots strengthen brand recognition.

**Color Psychology:**
- Blue: Trust, stability (finance, education)
- Green: Growth, safety (health, environment)
- Orange: Energy, fun (entertainment, gaming)
- Pink: Warmth, approachability (lifestyle)

### 4. Safe Area Compliance

Position critical content away from notches and home indicators:

**Safe Zone Requirements:**
- Top: 50-60px (notch + status bar)
- Bottom: 34-40px (home indicator)
- Left/right: 16-20px each side

## Recommended Hybrid Approach for Rork Apps

For Rork-generated apps, **combining Figma and Canva** is optimal:

### Three-Phase Workflow

1. **Phase 1: Figma Template Creation**
   - Time: 60 minutes
   - Build brand-compliant template
   - Benefit: Reusable, branded

2. **Phase 2: Canva for Multiple Languages**
   - Time: 8 minutes per language
   - Fast localization using templates
   - Benefit: Efficient scaling

3. **Phase 3: Figma Quality Check**
   - Time: 5 minutes per language
   - Final brand consistency verification
   - Benefit: Ensures quality

**Example: Monthly Update Cycle**
- New screenshot design in Figma: 60 minutes
- 3-language rollout in Canva: 24 minutes
- 2 A/B variations in Canva: 10 minutes
- **Total: 94 minutes for 6 high-quality variants** (3 languages × 2 variations)

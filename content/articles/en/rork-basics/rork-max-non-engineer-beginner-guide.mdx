## FAQs: Common Worries for Non-Programmers

### Q: Do I really need zero programming knowledge?

**A: Absolutely.** Rork Max users are often designers, entrepreneurs, and business people. What matters isn't technical knowledge—it's having a **clear vision** of what your app should do.

When you give feedback, plain English works fine: "Move that to the center," "Make this button bigger," "Add a dark mode option."

### Q: Will a Rork Max app really get approved on the App Store?

**A: Yes. Rork Max automates the entire publishing process.** Normally, publishing an app requires:

- Apple Developer Program membership ($99/year)
- Certificate and code signing setup
- TestFlight beta testing
- App Store submission with screenshots and descriptions

**Rork Max does all of this automatically.** You just:

1. Click "Publish"
2. Sign in with your Apple ID
3. Wait (usually 1-3 hours for approval)

See "[Rork Max 2クリック App Store 公開ガイド](/articles/rork-ai/rork-max-2-click-publish-complete-guide)" for step-by-step details.

### Q: Can I build complex features like payments or user accounts?

**A: Completely.** Rork Max handles:

- **Stripe integration**: In-app purchases and subscriptions
- **Authentication**: Apple ID, Google, email sign-in
- **Firebase / Supabase**: Real-time databases
- **iCloud sync**: Data synced across user devices

The more specific your prompt, the better the result. Instead of "Add user accounts," try: "Users sign in with Apple ID, upload a profile photo, and can friend other users."

### Q: Do I need to manage a server?

**A: Not if you don't want to.** Rork Max apps support:

- **CloudKit** (Apple-managed): Zero server work
- **Firebase** (Google): Free tier handles most small apps
- **Supabase** (open-source): Affordable, developer-friendly

Pick CloudKit and Apple handles everything. You focus on features.

### Q: How hard is it to update my app?

**A: Easy.** With Rork Max:

1. Open your existing project
2. Describe the change you want
3. Get a new version in minutes
4. One click to publish an update

Traditional development means days of work for a single update. Rork Max? Hours, typically.

## Prompting Tips for Non-Engineers

### 1. Start Small

Your first app should be simple—a weather app, a to-do list, a habit tracker. Complex apps require more feedback loops and burn through credits faster.

### 2. Show, Don't Tell

Instead of "Make it look like Instagram," screenshot a real Instagram screen and upload it to Rork Max with "Like this design." Visual examples are worth a thousand words.

### 3. Describe the User Workflow

Vague: "Build a note-taking app"  
Clear: "Users tap a button to create a note, type text, save it to the device, and see a list of all saved notes with the most recent first"

### 4. Use the Feedback Form Liberally

Refinement requests are free. Don't hesitate to ask for spacing adjustments, color changes, or layout tweaks.

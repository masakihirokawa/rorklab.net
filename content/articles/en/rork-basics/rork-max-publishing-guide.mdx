Once you've built your app with Rork Max, the next crucial step is **publishing it to production**. This guide walks you through the entire process of taking your creation from development to a live service accessible to real users.

## What is Publishing in Rork Max?

Rork Max (formerly Rork AI) is a no-code platform for building web applications. When you publish an app, it becomes a live web service that anyone with the URL can access. Your code runs on Rork's infrastructure or your own servers, depending on your chosen method.

**Types of apps you can publish with Rork Max:**
- Customer relationship management (CRM) systems
- Inventory management tools
- Data analytics dashboards
- Content delivery platforms
- Personal portfolio websites

## Three Publishing Methods Explained

### 1. Quick Publish (Recommended for Beginners)

The simplest way to go live.

**Steps:**
1. Open your Rork Max project
2. Click the **"Publish"** button in the top-right corner
3. Confirm in the dialog that appears
4. A unique URL is automatically generated
5. Share this URL—your app is now live\!

**Advantages:**
- Zero setup required
- One-click deployment
- Automatic hosting on Rork's servers

**Disadvantages:**
- Uses a Rork subdomain (e.g., `yourapp.rorkapp.io`)
- Limited SEO capabilities

### 2. Custom Domain Publishing (For Professional Use)

Use your own domain name (e.g., `myapp.com`) for a branded presence.

**Requirements:**
- Registered domain (from GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.)
- Rork Max Pro plan or higher

**Step-by-step setup:**

```markdown
**Step 1: Add Domain to Rork Max**
1. Go to Rork Max Dashboard → Settings → Custom Domains
2. Click "Add Domain"
3. Enter your domain name (e.g., myapp.com)

**Step 2: Configure DNS**
- Copy the CNAME record provided by Rork Max
- Log in to your domain registrar's DNS panel
- Add a new CNAME record:
  - Name: @ or www
  - Value: [the CNAME from Rork Max]
- Wait 1-24 hours for propagation

**Step 3: SSL Certificate Auto-Issuance**
- Rork Max automatically issues a free Let's Encrypt certificate
- Your site will be HTTPS-secure within hours
```

**Advantages:**
- Professional branding
- Better SEO performance
- Increased user trust

**Disadvantages:**
- Domain registration costs ($10-50/year)
- Requires basic DNS knowledge
- Propagation delays (1-24 hours)

### 3. Self-Hosting (For Advanced Users)

Deploy to your own server infrastructure.

**Compatible platforms:**
- Vercel (recommended for Next.js-based apps)
- Netlify
- AWS Amplify
- Heroku

**Advantages:**
- Complete control over infrastructure
- Highly scalable
- Unlimited customization

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher technical complexity
- Server maintenance costs
- Significant management overhead

## Common Publishing Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Custom Domain Not Resolving After Setup

**Cause:** DNS propagation in progress or misconfiguration

**Solutions:**
- Wait 24 hours for full propagation
- Clear browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Delete)
- Try a different browser or incognito mode
- Use `nslookup myapp.com` to verify DNS records

### Issue 2: 403 Forbidden Error on Published App

**Cause:** Incorrect access permissions in Rork Max

**Solutions:**
1. Go to Rork Max Settings → Access Settings
2. Change to "Public" or "Anyone with link"
3. Clear browser cache and retry

### Issue 3: Images Not Loading on Published App

**Cause:** Image URLs using relative paths instead of absolute URLs

**Solutions:**
- Use images uploaded directly to Rork Max
- Ensure external images use HTTPS URLs
- Check image permissions in your asset library

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can I unpublish my app after launching?**

A: Yes. Go to Settings and switch to "Private" mode. The app becomes inaccessible to the public.

**Q2: Do I need a paid plan to publish?**

A: No. You can use Quick Publish on the free plan. Custom domains require Pro or higher.

**Q3: Will my app update in real-time if I make changes?**

A: Yes. Changes you make in the editor reflect immediately on your published app.

**Q4: Are there limits on how many users can access my app?**

A: Limits depend on your Rork Max plan. See the [pricing page](https://rork.com/pricing) for details.

**Q5: Can I publish the same app on multiple domains?**

A: Yes. Pro plans and above support multiple custom domains for the same app.

## Your "I Wish" Moments Can Become Real Apps

In our previous article, "[Your First App in 30 Minutes](/en/articles/rork-basics/rork-first-app-30min)," you built your first app and discovered something important: it's easier than you thought!

Today, we'll explore everyday situations where you might think "I wish there was an app for that" — and show you exactly how to ask Rork to build each one.

## Health and Wellness Apps

### Blood Pressure Tracker

```
You: "Build an app to record daily blood pressure
      (systolic, diastolic) and pulse.
      - Auto-fill date and time
      - Show past records in a list
      - Graph showing trends over time
      - Large, easy-to-read text"
```

Bring your records to doctor appointments for more productive conversations.

### Medication Reminder

```
You: "Build a medication reminder app.
      - Set 4 daily time slots (morning, noon, evening, bedtime)
      - Check off each dose when taken
      - Show missed doses
      - Weekly compliance overview"
```

### Exercise Log

```
You: "An app to log daily steps and walk duration.
      - Manual number entry
      - Weekly and monthly summaries
      - Set a daily goal with celebration message when met"
```

## Family Connection Apps

### Shared Family Calendar

```
You: "Build a family calendar app.
      - Color-code each family member
      - Enter event name, date/time, and notes
      - Show today's and tomorrow's events at the top"
```

### Grandchild Growth Tracker

```
You: "Build an app to track my grandchild's growth.
      - Register name and birthday
      - Log height and weight monthly
      - Record milestones (first word, first steps, etc.)
      - Add photo notes"
```

### Check-In App for Elderly Parents

```
You: "An app for my mom who lives alone.
      - A big 'I'm doing well' button she presses daily
      - Optional one-line health note
      - View all entries in a list"
```

## Tips for Better AI Instructions

### Tip 1: Describe When and Where You'll Use It

```
△ "Make a list app"
◎ "Make a grocery list app I'll use
   while shopping at the supermarket"
```

### Tip 2: Describe Who Will Use It

```
"This is for seniors in their 70s, so please use
 large text, big buttons, and simple navigation."
```

### Tip 3: Add Features Gradually

Start simple, use the app for a while, then ask for new features as you discover what you need.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Where is app data stored?

It depends on how the app is built. Data can be stored on your phone or in the cloud. Ask AI to "save data to the cloud" if you want it accessible across devices.

### Q: How do I teach my family to use the app?

Keep it simple! Aim for "3 buttons or fewer" and "1-2 screens." A well-designed simple app needs no instruction manual.

### Q: Can I change how the app looks?

"Change the color to pink," "Add a floral background," "Make the font rounder" — just describe what you want and AI will update the design.

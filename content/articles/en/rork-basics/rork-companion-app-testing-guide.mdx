You've built something in Rork and the simulator looks fine — but you want to see how it actually feels in your hand before sharing it with anyone.

That's exactly what the **Rork Companion app** is for. It lets you preview your Rork project on a real iPhone or iPad without needing an Apple Developer account, Xcode, or a TestFlight build. Here's how to set it up and what to actually check once you're running on device.

## What the Companion App Does

Rork Companion is an official app available on the App Store. You open it on your device, scan a QR code from your Rork project, and the app loads directly on your phone.

**Why real device testing matters**

The simulator runs on your Mac and approximates what the phone looks like. The real device is where your users will actually be. Key differences:

- **Camera**: Doesn't work in the simulator. On a real device, you can test camera access and see actual permissions prompts
- **Touch feel**: Scrolling, swiping, and tap accuracy behave differently when you're using your actual fingers
- **Performance**: The simulator uses your Mac's chip. The phone uses an A-series chip. Animations and transitions can feel different
- **Push notifications**: Limited in the simulator, fully functional on device
- **Sensors**: Accelerometer, gyroscope, GPS are simulated values in the simulator, real data on device

## Setup: Three Steps

**Step 1: Install Rork Companion on your iPhone or iPad**

Search "Rork Companion" in the App Store and install it. You can also find the link on [Rork's official website](https://rork.app).

**Step 2: Open your project in Rork and get the QR code**

In Rork (desktop app or web), open your project and look for the preview or share options. Find "Open on device" or "Companion" — this will display a QR code.

**Step 3: Scan the QR code in the Companion app**

Open Companion on your phone, tap "Scan QR Code," and point it at your screen. Your app should load within a few seconds.

No accounts, no certificates, no build configurations needed.

## What to Check on the Real Device

Running on device isn't just about confirming it looks the same. These are the things worth checking specifically:

**Tap target sizes**

You click with a mouse in the simulator. You tap with a finger on the real device. Apple recommends a minimum tap target size of 44×44 points. If something feels hard to tap, that's a real usability problem worth fixing before launch.

**Scroll and swipe behavior**

Momentum scrolling on a real device feels different from the simulator's approximation. Test any list views, carousels, or swipe interactions on device before finalizing them.

**Notch and Dynamic Island layout**

Different iPhone models have different notch sizes or Dynamic Island shapes. Content that looks fine in the simulator can end up obscured on device. Check the top of the screen carefully on whichever models you have access to.

**Permission dialogs**

If your app requests camera, location, or microphone access, the permission dialog only fully appears on a real device. Confirm the dialog text makes sense in context — this is often the first thing a new user reads.

## Common Issues and Fixes

**QR code scans but nothing loads**

- Make sure your project is saved and the build has completed in Rork
- Confirm your iPhone and Mac are on the same Wi-Fi network
- Force-close Companion and try again

**Layout looks different from the simulator**

The simulator is an approximation. If real-device rendering differs, treat the real device as the source of truth and adjust your layout to match.

**App crashes on a specific device**

Memory and CPU constraints differ across iPhone generations. If older devices crash, check whether any images or animations are particularly heavy. Rork's community forums are a good place to report device-specific issues.

## Companion vs. Final Device Testing

The Companion app is a preview tool, not a substitute for full device testing. Before submitting to the App Store, you'll want to run more thorough tests — including TestFlight builds on multiple devices.

But for the "does this actually feel good to use?" question, Companion is the fastest answer. If you haven't tried it on a real device yet, scan a QR code now before you go any further with the design.

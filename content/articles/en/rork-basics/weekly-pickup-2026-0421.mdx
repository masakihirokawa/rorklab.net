## #1: An Honest One-Year Review of Rork — What It's Good At, What It Struggles With, and Whether It's Worth It

A flat, unvarnished take from someone who has used Rork daily for a year. It earned a strong response from readers trying to make a real subscription decision and was widely quoted on social.

The standout passage names what Rork is genuinely fast at, rather than claiming the tool is universally great. Side by side, the author lists the daily moments where Rork pulls ahead, and the moments where switching to Cursor or Claude Code was simply quicker. That balance is rare and makes the article useful as a decision aid.

Read [An Honest One-Year Rork Review](/articles/rork-basics/rork-honest-year-review-2026) and use it to gauge fit with your own development style.

## #2: Shipping a Wallpaper App to the App Store with Rork — The Full Record from Idea to Release

A case study following a wallpaper app from concept through implementation, submission, and release. It drew an exceptionally strong response from solo developers in the wallpaper genre — overlapping with the author's own experience running wallpaper apps — because the decision points are concrete, not theoretical.

What makes the article work is that it doesn't celebrate the finished product; it quietly logs the places that got stuck and how they were unstuck. If you've never shipped a release, treat the obstacles described here as obstacles you will also hit — and let them shape your prep time.

See the full record at [Shipping a Wallpaper App to the App Store with Rork](/articles/app-dev/rork-wallpaper-app-from-zero-to-store).

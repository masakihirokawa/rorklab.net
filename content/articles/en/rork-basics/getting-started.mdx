## Welcome to Rork

Building a mobile app has never been easier. In the next five minutes, you'll create a fully functional mobile application without writing any code. This guide walks you through every step.

## Step 1: Create Your Account

**Time: 1 minute**

1. Visit the Rork website
2. Click "Sign Up" or "Get Started"
3. Choose your sign-up method:
   - Email and password
   - Google account
   - Apple ID
   - Other social authentication options
4. Verify your email address
5. You're in!

You now have access to the Rork platform. By default, you'll be on a free tier that allows you to experiment with a limited number of prompts.

## Step 2: Select Your Plan

**Time: 1 minute**

### Free Tier

The free tier is perfect for experimenting:

- Approximately 5 prompts per week to generate apps
- Full preview and testing capabilities
- Ability to download generated code
- Access to all basic features
- Great for learning and trying out ideas

**Best for**: Students, experimenters, and those testing the waters

### Paid Plans

Rork offers paid subscription plans for serious app development:

- **Rork Pro**: $25-50/month (depending on your specific needs)
- **Rork Max**: $200/month for native Swift iOS development
- **Unlimited prompts**: Generate as many apps as you want
- **Priority support**: Faster responses to questions
- **Commercial use**: Build and sell apps built with Rork

<Callout type="info">
You can stay on the free tier to complete this guide. Upgrade later when you're ready to build more apps.
</Callout>

## Step 3: Start Your First Project

**Time: 1 minute**

1. From your dashboard, click "Create New App" or "Start Generating"
2. You'll see a text input field or prompt interface
3. This is where you describe your app idea

## Step 4: Write an Effective Prompt

**Time: 1-2 minutes**

This is where your creativity comes in. Your prompt should describe:

- **What type of app**: ("A weather app", "A task manager", "A recipe search tool")
- **Main features**: ("Shows current weather and 7-day forecast", "Users can add tasks and mark them complete")
- **Who uses it**: ("For busy professionals", "For home cooks")
- **Key information displayed**: ("Temperature, humidity, wind speed", "Task title, due date, priority level")

### Example Prompts

**Simple Prompt**:
> "Create a simple counter app where users can increment and decrement a number with large buttons."

**Detailed Prompt**:
> "Build a habit tracking app where users can log daily habits, see a calendar view of their streak, and get encouragement when they complete habits."

**Advanced Prompt**:
> "Create a note-taking app with categories, search functionality, dark mode, and ability to export notes as text files. Users should be able to create, edit, delete, and organize notes."

### Tips for Better Prompts

**Be Specific**: Instead of "create a weather app," say "create a weather app that shows current temperature, humidity, wind speed, and a 7-day forecast with icons."

**Describe the User Experience**: Think about how users interact with your app. "Users tap buttons to navigate between screens" or "Users swipe through a card-based interface."

**Mention Visual Style**: Suggest design preferences like "modern and minimalist," "colorful and playful," or "professional and corporate."

**Include Data**:  Mention if your app needs to save data: "Users can create tasks and they're saved locally," or "The app fetches weather data from a weather API."

**Example Strong Prompt**:
> "Build a hydration reminder app that sends notifications reminding users to drink water every hour. The app should show how much water they've drunk today with a visual progress bar. Users can customize the reminder frequency. Use a blue and white color scheme with a modern, clean design."

<Callout type="tip">
Start simple for your first app. As you get comfortable with Rork, experiment with more complex features.
</Callout>

## Step 5: Generate Your App

**Time: 1-2 minutes**

1. Type or paste your prompt into the text field
2. Click "Generate," "Create App," or similar button
3. Rork's AI springs into action:
   - Analyzing your description
   - Generating code
   - Building your app
   - Preparing the preview
4. This typically takes 30-90 seconds
5. You'll see a loading indicator while the AI works

## Step 6: Preview Your App

### For Rork (React Native)

**Using Expo Go** (Recommended for Testing):

1. A preview will appear on screen showing your app
2. A QR code will be generated
3. On your phone, open the Expo Go app (download free from App Store or Google Play)
4. Scan the QR code with your phone camera
5. Your app launches on your device instantly
6. Test all the features, buttons, and interactions

**In Browser Preview**:
- A simulator view shows what your app looks like
- You can interact with it but experience is limited on desktop
- Use the phone preview for better testing

### For Rork Max (Native Swift)

1. The streaming simulator preview loads in your browser
2. See a live preview of your iOS app running
3. No need to download anything—it streams directly
4. Interact with the simulator in real-time
5. See changes instantly as you modify your app

<Callout type="info">
The streaming simulator in Rork Max eliminates the need to own a Mac or deal with Xcode compilation.
</Callout>

## Step 7: Test and Iterate

**Time: 1-3 minutes**

Now comes the fun part—testing your creation:

**On Your Device**:
- Tap buttons and interact with the interface
- Test all features you described
- Look for any issues or unexpected behavior
- See how the app responds to your inputs

**Provide Feedback**:
If something isn't quite right, you can refine it:

1. Go back to the prompt
2. Modify it with specific feedback
3. Examples:
   - "Add a save button to the top right"
   - "Change the background color to light blue"
   - "Add a search feature to find items"
   - "The buttons are too small—make them bigger"
4. Click "Regenerate" or "Refine"
5. Rork creates an updated version based on your feedback

The iterative process is one of Rork's superpowers. Keep refining until your app matches your vision.

## Step 8: Download or Deploy

**Time: 1-5 minutes (varies by platform)**

### Downloading Your Project

Once you're happy with your app:

**For Rork**:
1. Click "Download Code" or "Export Project"
2. Your app's source code downloads as a zip file
3. You get the complete React Native project
4. You can:
   - Continue building locally with React Native tools
   - Deploy to App Store and Google Play yourself
   - Share the code with developers for further customization

**For Rork Max**:
1. Click "Publish to App Store" or similar
2. Rork Max handles the compilation in the cloud
3. Submit to App Store (2-click process)
4. No Xcode installation required

### App Store Deployment

**Getting Your App Published** (Longer process, but Rork helps):

1. Create Apple Developer and/or Google Play Developer accounts ($99/year for Apple, $25 one-time for Google)
2. Prepare app store listings:
   - Description
   - Screenshots
   - Icon
   - Privacy policy
   - Keywords/categories
3. For Rork: Submit your app using standard tools
4. For Rork Max: Use the 2-click submission (much simpler)
5. Wait for app review (typically 24 hours for Google, 1-3 days for Apple)
6. Your app appears in app stores

## Step 9: Share and Celebrate

Once your app is created, you can:

- **Share the QR code** with friends to test it
- **Deploy to app stores** to reach millions of users
- **Download the code** to continue development
- **Create another app** using the same process

## Common Questions for Beginners

### "What if I make a mistake in my prompt?"

No problem! You can:
- Edit your prompt and regenerate
- Refine the app with follow-up prompts
- Delete and start over
- Keep the old version and try something new

### "Can I combine my app with my own backend?"

Absolutely! Rork supports:
- Supabase
- Firebase
- Airtable
- Custom APIs
- Your own backend

Mention in your prompt that you want to integrate a specific backend.

### "Is the generated code actually usable?"

Yes! Rork generates production-ready code. You can:
- Deploy directly to app stores
- Customize it further with traditional development
- Share it with developers for improvements
- Use it as a starting point for something bigger

### "What languages does Rork support?"

Rork supports:
- **Rork (original)**: React Native generates JavaScript/TypeScript code
- **Rork Max**: Native Swift code

Both generate industry-standard code using best practices.

### "How do I update my app after launching?"

Simple:
1. Go back to Rork
2. Modify your prompt with new features
3. Regenerate the app
4. Download the new version
5. Deploy the update to app stores

## Tips for Success

<Callout type="tip">
**Start Small**: Your first app should be simple. A counter, a to-do list, or a simple quiz. Master the basics before building complex apps.
</Callout>

**Clear Descriptions Matter**: The better you describe your app, the better the result. Include details about colors, button placement, and user flows.

**Test Thoroughly**: Before deploying to app stores, test all features on a real device. Make sure buttons work, data saves, and the experience is smooth.

**Iterate Openly**: Don't be afraid to regenerate your app multiple times. Rork is designed for rapid iteration.

**Learn from Results**: After generating your first app, look at the code (if you download it). You'll start understanding how modern apps work.

**Community and Resources**: Check Rork's documentation and community forums for examples and inspiration.

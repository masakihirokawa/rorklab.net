## #1: The Complete Rork Max SwiftUI Native App Implementation Guide

This is a hands-on examination of how far you can push Rork Max when building a SwiftUI-based native iOS app. The article walks through the journey from scaffolding to a polished, native-feeling app — the kind of read that quietly changes the mind of anyone who assumed Rork was limited to "WebView wrappers."

Readers especially valued the sections on the unglamorous details: safe-area handling, keyboard avoidance, dark-mode polish — the parts that actually decide whether an app feels native. If you care about iOS-style finishing on Apple platforms, the article gives you both the implementation paths and the polish patterns.

Read more in [The Complete Rork Max SwiftUI Native App Implementation Guide](/articles/rork-ai/rork-max-swiftui-complete-implementation-guide).

## #2: The Rork Max TipKit Guide — In-App Guidance and Onboarding That Sticks

A guide to integrating Apple's TipKit framework into Rork Max apps, with patterns for in-app guidance and onboarding. One of the most painful indie-development failures is shipping an app where users never discover the features that matter most — TipKit is a lightweight way to prevent that quietly.

The article includes implementation examples for tip display conditions, frequency control, and A/B testing of guidance flows. If retention on your Rork Max apps feels lower than it should, this is something to fold in before your next release.

Implementation in [The Rork Max TipKit Guide](/articles/rork-dev/rork-max-tipkit-in-app-guidance-onboarding-complete-guide).

## #3: Comparing Rork App Revenue Models — Ads, Subscription, or One-Time Purchase?

A thoughtful comparison of three monetization shapes for Rork-built apps: ads, subscription, and one-time purchase. Rather than the lazy "AI-built apps must be cheap" frame, the article matches each model to the experience and value structure it actually fits.

It goes into revenue curves, support cost, and exit criteria — the kind of detail that makes a real difference when you're shipping multiple apps in parallel. If you've been running on the same monetization model out of habit, this is a useful nudge to revisit it.

Read the full breakdown in [Comparing Rork App Revenue Models](/articles/rork-business/rork-app-revenue-model-comparison-ads-subscription-onetime).

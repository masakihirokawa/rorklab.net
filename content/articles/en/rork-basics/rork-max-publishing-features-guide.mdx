You've built an amazing app in Rork Max, but now comes the real challenge: **getting it published on the App Store and Google Play**. Unlike some no-code tools, Rork Max doesn't automate the entire submission process. Instead, it gives you the IPA and APK files—and hands you the keys to publish them yourself.

This article walks you through the actual publishing workflow, from your first build to seeing your app in the store. We'll cover what Rork Max does, what you need to do, and the pitfalls that trip up most developers.

## What Rork Max Publishing Really Does

Rork Max builds your app into **IPA files (iOS) and APK files (Android)** that are ready for submission. Here's what it doesn't do:

- ❌ Submit directly to App Store Connect
- ❌ Upload directly to Google Play Console
- ❌ Handle certification or code signing
- ✅ Generate production-ready binaries
- ✅ Let you customize build settings
- ✅ Support multi-platform simultaneous release

**Why this design?** Because you need control. App Store and Google Play each have specific requirements, and automating away that step would mean sacrificing flexibility for convenience.

## Step-by-Step Publishing Workflow

### Step 1: Verify Your App Settings

Before you build, make sure these are correct in Rork Max:

- **App Name** — What appears on the App Store (15 characters max recommended)
- **Bundle ID** — Unique identifier (`com.yourname.appname`)
- **Version** — Usually `1.0.0` for first release
- **Build Number** — Sequential integer (1, 2, 3...)
- **App Icon** — 1024×1024 PNG, no transparency, no rounded corners

```
⚠️ WARNING: Bundle ID cannot be changed after your first submission.
If you get it wrong, you'll have to create an entirely new app listing.
```

### Step 2: Build Your App

In Rork Max dashboard:

1. Click **"Build"** or **"Publish"**
2. Select your platform(s): iOS only, Android only, or both
3. Wait 5–10 minutes for the build to complete
4. Download the IPA (iOS) and/or APK (Android) files

These files are your submission artifacts. Keep them safe—they're the actual app you'll upload to the stores.

### Step 3: Test on TestFlight (iOS)

**Do not skip this step.** Before submitting to App Store review, test your IPA on real devices via TestFlight:

1. Log into [App Store Connect](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com/)
2. Create a new app
3. Upload your IPA to the **Builds** section
4. Add yourself as an internal tester
5. Install the app on your iPhone via the TestFlight app
6. Test thoroughly—check for crashes, network issues, UI glitches

A build that crashes in TestFlight will be rejected by App Store review, wasting days of your time.

### Step 4: Test on Google Play Internal Testing (Android)

Same deal for Android:

1. Log into [Google Play Console](https://play.google.com/console)
2. Create a new app
3. Upload your APK to **Testing → Internal testing**
4. Add yourself as a tester
5. Install from Google Play Store app and verify everything works

### Step 5: Submit to App Store

Prepare this metadata:

- **Short description** (170 characters)
- **Keywords** (100 characters, comma-separated)
- **Screenshots** (5 recommended, 1242×2208 for iPhone)
- **Privacy Policy URL** (must be a real HTTPS link)
- **Support email**
- **Age rating** (4+, 12+, 17+)

Upload these in App Store Connect, then click **"Submit for Review."** You'll get a decision in 24–48 hours.

### Step 6: Submit to Google Play

Similar process, but with more room for descriptions:

- **Full description** (up to 4,000 characters)
- **Screenshots** (2–5)
- **Privacy Policy** (if you collect data)
- **Promo video** (optional)
- **Content rating questionnaire** (auto-filled)

Google Play often approves within hours, sometimes auto-publishes immediately.

## Common Publishing Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: No Pre-Release Testing

Submitting directly to App Store without TestFlight testing almost always ends in rejection. You'll waste a week going back and forth.

**Fix:** Always test on real devices in TestFlight first. Period.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Changing the Bundle ID

App Store ties everything to your bundle ID—reviews, ratings, update history. Change it after launch and you're starting a new app from zero.

**Fix:** Get your bundle ID right the first time. Check it three times before building.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Wrong Icon Dimensions

Your 1024×1024 PNG app icon must be exactly that size—no JPEG, no transparency, no rounded corners (App Store adds them). Mismatched icons cause submission rejections.

**Fix:** Export from Figma as PNG, 100% scale, 1024×1024.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Missing or Broken Privacy Policy

Both App Store and Google Play require a privacy policy URL. "We don't collect data" doesn't mean you can skip it.

**Fix:** Create a simple one-page policy on Notion or GitHub Pages, include the URL.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Mismatched Versions

If you submit version 1.0.0 to iOS and 1.0.1 to Android, you've created confusion. Keep versions in sync across platforms.

**Fix:** Build both IPA and APK in the same Rork Max session, increment versions together.

## Simple Privacy Policy Template

Both stores require a privacy policy. Here's a minimal template:

```
Privacy Policy for [App Name]

This app collects the following information:

1. Location data (if location features are used)
2. Photos (if upload features are used)
3. User input text (if stored on servers)

This information is stored on [your server/Firebase/Supabase] 
and is never shared with third parties.

Users can revoke permissions in Settings at any time.

Last updated: April 14, 2026
```

Host this on Notion, GitHub Pages, or any HTTPS URL. The stores don't care about design—they just need the URL.

## Updating Your App After Launch

Once live, you'll release updates regularly:

- **1.0.1** — Bug fixes
- **1.1.0** — New features
- **2.0.0** — Major redesign

For each update:
1. Modify code in Rork Max
2. Increment version number
3. Build → Download IPA/APK
4. Re-upload to App Store Connect / Google Play Console

Both stores let you submit unlimited updates, so iterate freely.

## Future of Rork Max Publishing

Rook Max may eventually add:
- Direct App Store Connect integration
- Automated Google Play uploads

But today, it stops at build generation. Everything after that is your responsibility—and that's actually a good thing, because it keeps you in control.

## Summary

Rork Max's publishing approach isn't trying to be a full CI/CD pipeline for app stores. Instead, it focuses on what it does best: **generating production-ready binaries quickly.**

You handle the rest:
- Testing via TestFlight and internal testing
- Metadata preparation (icons, screenshots, descriptions)
- Store submission and compliance
- Version management and updates

This separation of concerns means you keep the flexibility of native development while enjoying the speed of no-code building. It's the best of both worlds.

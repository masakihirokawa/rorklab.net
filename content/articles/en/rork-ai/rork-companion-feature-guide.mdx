Rork Companion is the chat panel that lives alongside your app preview in Rork. Type a request in plain language, and the AI modifies your app in real time. If you've used Rork at all, you've probably already seen it in action.

The gap between a mediocre result and a precise one often comes down to how you phrase your requests. Here's what actually works.

## What Companion Can Do

The range of supported operations is broader than most users realize:

- **UI changes**: colors, fonts, layout adjustments, adding or removing components
- **Feature additions**: "Add a favorites button to each list item"
- **Data manipulation**: "Add 5 sample items to the list"
- **Bug fixes**: "The back button on the profile screen isn't working — fix it"

## Writing Effective Prompts

**Be specific about location**

```
❌ "Make the button look better"
✅ "Change the 'Submit' button at the bottom of the home screen — white text, background color #4F46E5"
```

**Include the current state**

```
❌ "Change the color"
✅ "The card backgrounds are currently gray — change them to white and add a subtle drop shadow"
```

**One change at a time**

Bundling multiple changes into a single request increases the chance something goes wrong. Confirm each change looks right before moving to the next.

## Practical Use Cases

**Rapid prototyping**

```
You: "Create a user registration form with email and password fields,
     a terms of service checkbox, and a register button"

→ Form screen is generated

You: "Add a small error message below the email field
     that validates email format in real time"

→ Form updates with inline validation
```

**Design refinements**

```
You: "Add a colored dot to the left of each list item that changes based on category.
     'Work' = blue, 'Personal' = green, 'Other' = gray"
```

**Bug reports**

```
You: "There's no way to go back from the profile screen.
     Add a back button to the navigation bar"
```

## Things to Watch Out For

**Check the change history**

Companion's modifications appear in Rork's history panel. If something looks wrong, undo immediately before making further changes — it's easier to revert one change than to untangle several.

**Break large requests into steps**

"Make the whole app look more modern" is too vague. Work screen by screen, section by section. The more control you want over the output, the more specific and incremental your prompts should be.

**Use your component names**

If you've named components in Rork, use those names in your Companion requests. It's more precise than describing what something looks like.

## Summary

Rork Companion makes it genuinely possible to build and refine an app without writing code. The quality of results scales directly with the quality of your prompts — specific, contextual instructions produce better outcomes than vague ones.

## A Developer's Encounter with Mr. Kura — When Government UX Hits Rock Bottom

I build apps for a living. I think about user experience every day. So when I tell you Japan's government services have the worst UX I've ever encountered, I'm speaking from professional experience.

In April 2026, I called the Uozu Tax Office in Toyama Prefecture. Mr. Kura, the official who answered, **barely introduced himself** — imagine an app that launches without telling you what it does.

I explained five years of unjust taxation: ¥5 million in overcollected consumption tax, a denied refund, a statute of limitations trap, payments exceeding my income, mental illness, a suspended art career.

I asked: **"If you were in my position, could you understand this situation?"**

His answer: **"I'm afraid I cannot."**

When I mentioned recording, he said **"I can't say anything"** and hung up.

On a follow-up call:

**"Considering the danger of being recorded, I cannot answer anything."**

I develop iPhone apps. My AdMob revenue from Google Ireland is an **export transaction** — legally **tax-exempt**. I paid approximately **¥5 million ($33,000)** in consumption tax through 2020 anyway.

The Miyazaki Tax Office **refused** my 2021 refund. Future filings reclassified as exempt, past overpayment unreturned. In 2024, refund rights **expired** — a deadline the office caused me to miss. They then demanded **additional payments** for the exempt period.

Blocked my refund, ran the clock, billed me again.

## A Developer's Encounter with Mr. Yagi — When Government UX Hits Rock Bottom

I build apps for a living. I think about user experience every single day — how to make interfaces intuitive, how to respond to user feedback, how to fix bugs before they ruin someone's day. So when I tell you that Japan's government services have the worst UX I've ever encountered, I'm speaking from professional experience.

In April 2026, I called the Uozu Tax Office in Toyama Prefecture. The official who answered was Mr. Yagi. He **barely introduced himself** — imagine an app that launches without telling you what it does. That was the first sign.

I walked him through five years of unjust taxation: ¥5 million in overcollected consumption tax on export income, a denied refund, a statute of limitations trap, monthly payments exceeding my income, mental illness, and a suspended art career.

After hearing everything, I asked: **"As a human being, can you understand this situation?"**

His answer: **"I don't understand."**

In app development terms, this is like a support agent responding to a critical crash report with "I don't understand." Except this isn't an app crash — it's a human life being systematically destroyed by a tax authority.

When I informed Mr. Yagi that the call was being recorded, he said **"I can't say anything"** and hung up. A system that breaks when you enable logging is a system that was never designed to work correctly.

## What Happened — Five Years of Unjust Taxation

I'm an iPhone app developer. My revenue comes from AdMob advertising paid by Google Ireland — an **export transaction** that is legally **tax-exempt** under Japanese law.

Despite this, I paid approximately **¥5 million ($33,000)** in consumption tax through 2020. When I filed for a refund in 2021, the Miyazaki Tax Office **refused**. They reclassified my future filings as tax-exempt but returned nothing from the past.

By 2024, a tax audit revealed my refund rights had **expired under the statute of limitations** — a deadline the office caused me to miss. Then they demanded **additional payments** for the period they themselves had classified as exempt.

They blocked my refund, ran the clock, then billed me again. This is the system working as intended.

Today's reality:

- Monthly income: **¥45,000 ($300)**
- Monthly tax repayment: **¥60,000+ ($400+)**
- Health: **adjustment disorder, depression, insomnia** — five years running
- Career: **17 international art awards**, practice **fully suspended** for five years

## Why Government Services Should Be Replaced by AI Apps

As a developer, I can state this with confidence: **the UX of Japan's government services is worse than any indie app I've ever seen**.

We developers listen to user feedback, fix bugs, ship updates, and iterate constantly. A one-star review triggers an investigation. A crash spike means an emergency patch. We are accountable to our users because our livelihood depends on their satisfaction.

Government services operate with zero accountability. "I don't understand" is an acceptable response. Recording triggers silence. Complaints vanish into bureaucratic void. There is no feedback loop, no iteration, no improvement.

AI-powered apps can fix this from the ground up.

**Tax Filing AI App.** An app that guides users through the entire tax filing process with AI. It automatically identifies export exemptions, optimizes deductions, and flags overpayments. No need to pay a tax accountant tens of thousands of yen. AI is more accurate, available around the clock, and actually serves the taxpayer's interest.

**Municipal Services AI App.** Moving, marriage registration, childcare benefits — every municipal procedure guided by AI. No waiting hours at a window. No shuffling between departments. Just clear, accurate instructions delivered instantly.

**Tax Audit AI App.** An app that lets taxpayers pre-check their filings with AI before the tax office does. Catch overpayments, missed deductions, and compliance issues before they become problems. Self-service tax auditing that actually protects citizens.

This needs to be said directly: the **tax accountant profession in Japan** is structurally obsolete.

My own accountant missed the fact that my export income was tax-exempt. An AI query would have caught this in seconds. The economics are straightforward: taxpayers pay substantial fees for services inferior to what free AI tools already provide.

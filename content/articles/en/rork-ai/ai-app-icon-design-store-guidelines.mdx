## App Store Icon Specifications & Requirements

Apple's App Store has precise icon requirements that vary by context. Getting these details right prevents rejection and ensures your icon looks sharp on all devices.

### iOS App Icon Sizes

For iOS, you need icons at multiple resolutions:

- **1024×1024 px** — App Store (primary submission)
- **180×180 px** — iPhone 6+, 7+, 8+, X home screen
- **167×167 px** — iPad Pro (2nd generation)
- **152×152 px** — iPad (2nd generation), iPad (3rd generation), iPad (4th generation), iPad (5th generation) home screen
- **120×120 px** — iPhone 6, 7, 8 home screen
- **80×80 px** — iPad mini, iPad mini 2, iPad mini 3, iPad mini 4 home screen
- **76×76 px** — iPad (non-retina) home screen
- **40×40 px** — iPad (non-retina) Spotlight search

Apple requires a single 1024×1024 px image during submission. The system automatically scales this for all other sizes, so quality matters at the highest resolution.

### Icon Design Requirements for Apple

- **No transparency** on the outer square (fill to edges)
- **No text** or app name embedded in the icon
- **Clean, simple design** — avoid complex details that don't scale
- **Avoid app category symbols** (camera, phone, note icons if not core to your brand)
- **Safe zone**: Keep important elements within the center 88% of the canvas
- **File format**: PNG or JPEG, 8-bit color (or 16-bit for advanced cases)

### Google Play Store Icon Specifications

Google Play requirements differ slightly and often feel more flexible, but precision is still critical.

- **512×512 px** — Main icon submission (PNG with 32-bit color)
- **192×192 px** — Large app icon display
- **108×108 px** — Home screen icon (Android 12+)
- **81×81 px** — Home screen icon (older Android versions)
- **48×48 px** — Small launcher icon
- **32×32 px** — Tiny launcher icon

Google requires the 512×512 px version during submission, similar to Apple.

### Icon Design Requirements for Google Play

- **PNG with 32-bit color** (transparency supported)
- **Safe zone**: Keep important elements within the center 66% of the canvas
- **No rounded corners** — Google applies these automatically based on device OEM customization
- **Simple, distinctive design** — clarity at small sizes is essential
- **Avoid text** or app branding within the icon shape
- **Transparent backgrounds** are acceptable and often encouraged

## Generating Icons with AI Tools & Rork

AI image generation APIs make it easy to create icon concepts. Here's how to approach this using Rork with prompt engineering.

### Crafting Effective Icon Prompts

A strong icon prompt balances detail with flexibility. Here's an example prompt structured for AI generation:

```
A professional app icon for a task management application.
Design features: A clean checkmark in the center, soft gradient background
(blue to purple), modern minimalist style, no text,
high contrast for visibility at small sizes, flat design aesthetic,
rounded corners not required, suitable for both iOS and Android,
professional and trustworthy appearance.
Dimensions consideration: Will scale from 512x512 down to 32x32,
so avoid fine details. Use bold shapes and clear colors.
Color palette: Primary blue (#2563EB), secondary purple (#9333EA),
white accents. Style: Apple iOS aesthetic but platform-agnostic.
```

**Key elements in an effective icon prompt:**
1. **App purpose** — What does the app do?
2. **Visual concept** — What symbol or metaphor represents it?
3. **Style** — Modern, flat, gradient, minimalist, playful?
4. **Color guidance** — Specific colors or mood?
5. **Technical constraints** — Safe zones, scalability, transparency
6. **Platform context** — iOS, Android, or both?

### Using Rork AI for Icon Generation

If you're working within Rork's AI capabilities, you can structure icon generation like this:

```
// Rork prompt template for icon generation
{
  "purpose": "Generate an app icon",
  "app_name": "TaskFlow",
  "description": "Task management app with focus on productivity",
  "style_preferences": {
    "aesthetic": "minimalist",
    "color_scheme": "blue and teal gradient",
    "complexity": "simple shapes only"
  },
  "technical_specs": {
    "format": "PNG with transparent background",
    "primary_size": "1024x1024",
    "safe_zone_percentage": 88
  },
  "additional_context": "Avoid complex gradients, ensure clarity at 32x32 size"
}
```

This structured approach helps AI models understand your constraints and generate production-ready assets faster.

### Iterating on Generated Icons

The first generation is rarely perfect. Use AI iteration to refine:

- **Prompt adjustments** — If colors feel off, specify exact hex codes
- **Concept pivots** — Try variations: "Same design but with a different primary shape"
- **Comparative requests** — "Generate three versions: one with a checkmark, one with a star, one with a lightbulb"

AI models respond well to iterative feedback. Each generation teaches the model more about your vision.

## App Icon Design Best Practices

Beyond meeting technical requirements, these design principles help your icon succeed in crowded app stores.

### 1. Clarity at All Sizes

Test your icon at the smallest size (32×32 px for iOS, 48×48 px for Android). If you can't instantly recognize what your icon represents at thumbnail size, it's too detailed.

**Practice**: Zoom your 1024×1024 icon to 10% size and view it from arm's length. If the concept is still clear, you're good.

### 2. Distinctive Color Strategy

Avoid generic blue or red—choose colors that visually distinguish your app in a grid of other apps. Complementary colors (opposite on the color wheel) often work well.

### 3. Consistency with Your Brand

Your app icon is a brand ambassador. If your website uses a specific color palette or shape language, echo that in your icon.

### 4. Avoid Trendy Elements

Icon trends change quickly. Designs with overly specific gradients or effects can feel dated within a year. Minimalist, geometric approaches age better.

### 5. Safe Zone Awareness

Always respect the safe zone (center 88% for iOS, center 66% for Google Play). Platform-specific system elements (badges, notifications) occupy corners, so important design elements must stay centered.

### 6. Accessibility Considerations

High contrast between foreground and background helps users with low vision. Aim for at least 3:1 contrast ratio between the primary design element and its background.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Do I need to submit different icons for iOS and Android, or can I use the same design?

**A**: You can use the same design concept for both platforms. However, iOS icons typically fill the entire square, while Google Play icons may benefit from transparent backgrounds with clear center focus. It's perfectly acceptable to submit the same icon to both platforms—just ensure it meets each platform's technical specifications (color depth, transparency, safe zones).

### Q: What if Apple or Google rejects my AI-generated icon?

**A**: Rejections are usually due to guideline violations rather than the icon being AI-generated. Common reasons include: text embedded in the icon, transparent outer edges, designs too similar to existing popular apps, or elements that look like system icons. Review the specific feedback from Apple or Google, adjust the prompt or design accordingly, and resubmit. You can typically resubmit within 24 hours.

### Q: Should I use gradients in my app icon?

**A**: Gradients can work, but use them thoughtfully. Complex gradients don't scale well to small sizes and can look muddy. If you use gradients, keep them subtle and ensure high contrast between gradient colors. Modern design trends favor solid colors or very gentle gradients. For AI generation, simply specify "subtle gradient" or "solid color" in your prompt to control this.

### Q: What resolution should I generate in Rork, and do I need to manually scale it down?

**A**: Always generate or submit at the largest required size (1024×1024 for iOS, 512×512 for Google Play). Your Rork build process should handle scaling down automatically. If it doesn't, use ImageMagick or a similar tool to create scaled versions:

```bash
# Scale a single icon down to multiple sizes
convert icon-1024.png -resize 180x180 icon-180.png
convert icon-1024.png -resize 120x120 icon-120.png
convert icon-1024.png -resize 80x80 icon-80.png
```

### Q: Can I use the same PNG file for both iOS and Android submissions?

**A**: Not directly. iOS requires PNG with no transparency (solid background), while Google Play recommends PNG with 32-bit color (transparency allowed). You'll need two versions: one solid-background version for iOS, and one with optional transparency for Android. However, your design concept and core visual can be identical—just adjust background and color specifications.

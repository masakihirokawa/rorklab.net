## Why TestFlight Matters Before You Ship

Once your Rork Max app runs cleanly in the simulator, the next critical step is **real-device beta testing**. TestFlight is Apple's official beta distribution platform — it lets you put your app in the hands of up to 10,000 testers before going through the full App Store review.

In this guide, you'll learn:

- How TestFlight works and the difference between internal and external testers
- How to register your app in App Store Connect
- How to upload a build directly from Rork Max
- How to invite and manage testers
- How to fix the most common upload errors
- How to run a tight weekly beta cycle that accelerates your release

**Who this is for**: Independent developers who built their first Rork Max app and are ready to test it on real devices.

## Prerequisites

### What You'll Need

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Apple Developer Program | $99/year (individual or organization) — required to distribute any app |
| App Store Connect account | Sign in with your Apple ID (automatically available with Developer Program) |
| Rork Max subscription | $200/month — required for native Swift builds |
| iOS device (optional) | Testers need a real device, but you can complete the setup without one |

> **Heads up**: Apple Developer Program registration can take a few hours to a full business day. Don't wait until the day before your planned beta launch to sign up.

## Understanding TestFlight: The Basics

TestFlight allows you to ship pre-release builds to testers without going through the full App Store review process.

### Internal vs. External Testers

| Type | Max Testers | How to Invite | App Review Required? |
|------|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Internal | 100 | Apple ID email (Developer Program members only) | No |
| External | 10,000 | Email or public link | First build only (Beta App Review) |

**Recommended flow for solo developers**: Start with internal testers to catch crashes, then open a public link to external testers for broader feedback — two stages, no surprises.

## Step 1: Register Your App in App Store Connect

### Create a New App Record

1. Go to [App Store Connect](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com) and sign in
2. Click "My Apps" → "+" → "New App"
3. Fill in the following fields:

```
Platform: iOS
Name: Your app's display name (can change later)
Primary Language: English (U.S.)
Bundle ID: The Bundle ID generated by Rork Max (e.g., net.rorklab.myapp)
SKU: A unique internal identifier (e.g., MYAPP001)
User Access: Full Access
```

> **How to find your Bundle ID in Rork Max**: Open your project → "Settings" → "App ID."

### Register the Bundle ID in Apple Developer Portal

Rork Max auto-generates an App ID, but you may need to manually register it on first use.

1. Visit [developer.apple.com/account](https://developer.apple.com/account)
2. Go to "Certificates, IDs & Profiles" → "Identifiers" → "+"
3. Select "App IDs" → "App"
4. Enter the exact Bundle ID from Rork Max (Explicit, not Wildcard)
5. Enable any required capabilities (Push Notifications, HealthKit, etc.) and save

## Step 2: Export and Upload Your IPA from Rork Max

Rork Max can build and upload your app to App Store Connect in two clicks — no Xcode needed.

```
// Rork Max export flow (conceptual — UI-based in the dashboard)

1. Open your project dashboard
2. Click "Deploy" → "TestFlight / App Store"
3. Confirm build settings:
   - Version number: 1.0.0 (example)
   - Build number: 1 (must increment on every upload)
4. Click "Build & Upload to App Store Connect"
5. Enter your Apple ID and App-Specific Password when prompted

// Expected output:
// ✅ Build succeeded
// ✅ IPA uploaded to App Store Connect
// ⏳ Processing... (typically 5–15 minutes)
```

> **Where to get your App-Specific Password**: Go to [appleid.apple.com](https://appleid.apple.com) → "Sign-In and Security" → "App-Specific Passwords" → Generate. Use this instead of your regular Apple ID password.

### After Upload: Confirm Processing

Once Rork Max reports a successful upload, switch to App Store Connect → "TestFlight" tab. Your build will show as "Processing." Wait until it changes to "Ready to Test" before inviting testers (usually 5–30 minutes).

## Step 3: Distribute to Internal Testers

### Create an Internal Test Group

1. In App Store Connect, go to your app → "TestFlight" tab
2. Under "Internal Testing," click "Create Group" (e.g., "Dev Team")
3. Add team members by their Apple ID email (they must be part of your Developer Program team)

### Assign a Build to the Group

1. Confirm the build shows "Ready to Test"
2. Click "Add Build" in your tester group and select the build
3. Invited testers receive an email — they install the free TestFlight app, accept the invite, and install your build in three taps

> Internal testers don't need Beta App Review approval. They can start testing immediately after the build finishes processing.

---

## Step 4: Distribute to External Testers via Public Link

External testing (up to 10,000 people) requires **Beta App Review** for the first build in each group — typically 24–48 hours. After that, you can push updated builds to the same group without re-review.

### Create an External Test Group

1. Under "External Testing," click "Create Group" (e.g., "Beta Users")
2. Enable "Public Link" — anyone with the URL can join your beta
3. Fill in the test information (what to test, feedback email)

### Submit for Beta App Review

```
// Pre-submission checklist for external testing
✅ Beta app description (tell testers what you want them to focus on)
✅ Feedback contact email
✅ No obvious guideline violations (mature content requires appropriate rating)
✅ No crash-heavy build (fix critical crashes in internal testing first)
✅ Encryption compliance info (required if app uses HTTPS — see FAQ)
```

Once approved, share your public TestFlight link on social media, a newsletter, or a landing page to recruit beta testers at scale.

## Common Errors and How to Fix Them

### Error 1: "Missing Compliance" Warning

**Cause**: Apps using standard encryption (HTTPS, SSL) require an export compliance declaration.

**Fix**:
1. In App Store Connect → your build → "Export Compliance Information"
2. Answer "Does your app use encryption?" → "Yes, but it uses only standard encryption"
3. The build activates immediately after answering

### Error 2: "This build has expired"

**Cause**: TestFlight builds automatically expire after 90 days.

**Fix**: Upload a new build from Rork Max with an incremented build number, then ask testers to reinstall from TestFlight.

### Error 3: Duplicate Build Number Rejected

**Cause**: Uploading the same version + build number combination more than once.

**Fix**:
```
// Build number must increment on every upload — even for the same app version
// Wrong
version: "1.0.0", build: "1" → fix and re-upload at build: "1" (❌)

// Correct
version: "1.0.0", build: "1" → fix and re-upload at build: "2" (✅)
version: "1.0.0", build: "2" → further fix → build: "3" (✅)
```

### Error 4: "Invalid Bundle ID"

**Cause**: Bundle ID mismatch between Rork Max and App Store Connect.

**Fix**: Verify the Bundle ID in App Store Connect → "App Information" and make sure it exactly matches (case-sensitive) the Bundle ID in your Rork Max project settings.

## Pro Tips: Running a Tight Beta Release Cycle

### Weekly Beta Cadence

Shipping weekly builds keeps testers engaged and builds momentum toward launch.

```
Monday:    Implement new features in Rork Max
Tuesday:   Upload build → distribute to internal testers → collect feedback
Wednesday: Fix crashes and UX issues flagged by internal testers
Thursday:  Upload fixed build → push to external testers via public link
Friday:    Analyze feedback → define priorities for next week
```

### Getting Quality Feedback

- TestFlight lets testers attach screenshots with annotations when reporting issues — enable this in your test group settings
- Crash reports appear automatically in App Store Connect → "Feedback"
- Consider setting up a Discord or Slack channel for your beta community — async discussion yields richer insights than in-app feedback alone

### Testing In-App Purchases in TestFlight

TestFlight uses Sandbox purchases, which are free and reset monthly. This means you can test your full subscription flow without charging testers.

```swift

// 2. On the test device: Settings → App Store → sign out and sign in
//    with the sandbox account
// 3. Trigger a purchase in the app — it processes instantly (no charge)

// Ask Rork Max AI:
// "Add RevenueCat debug logging enabled in sandbox mode only"
// → Expected output: Purchases.logLevel = .debug added under #if DEBUG block
```

## FAQ

### Q1: Is Beta App Review as strict as the regular App Store review?

No — Beta App Review is significantly lighter. It typically completes in 24–48 hours and focuses on safety (no malware, no obvious guideline violations) rather than full policy compliance. That said, avoid submitting crash-heavy builds. Fix known crashes internally before submitting for external review.

### Q2: Can testers use TestFlight for free?

Yes. The TestFlight app is free on the App Store. Testers don't need an Apple Developer account — just an Apple ID and the TestFlight app. Only the developer needs the paid Apple Developer Program membership.

### Q3: Can I use Rork Max's simulator instead of TestFlight for device testing?

Rork Max's cloud simulator covers most functionality. However, certain hardware features (camera, NFC, GPS accuracy, haptics, Face ID) only behave correctly on a real device. TestFlight is the easiest and most reliable way to test on real hardware.

### Q4: What happens when a tester's 90-day build expires?

The app stops launching on their device. You need to upload a new build from Rork Max (with an incremented build number). TestFlight notifies testers before expiry, but it's good practice to maintain regular build cadence to keep testers from hitting the deadline.

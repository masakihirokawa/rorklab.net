## Publishing Your Rork App to Google Play

Rork generates cross-platform apps using React Native and Expo, which means the same project can target both iOS and Android. Once you've built your app in Rork, publishing it to the Google Play Store is a straightforward process—if you know where to look.

This guide walks you through every step: from creating your Google Play Developer account to passing the review process and going live.

---

## Prerequisites

### Google Play Developer Account

You'll need a **Google Play Developer account** before you can publish anything. Here's what to know:

- **One-time registration fee**: $25 USD
- **Sign up at**: [Google Play Console](https://play.google.com/console)
- Use any Google account to register

> **Note**: Unlike the Apple Developer Program ($99/year), the Google Play fee is a one-time payment.

### Rork Project Requirements

Before exporting your build, confirm the following settings are in place in your Rork project:

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| App Name | The name displayed on the Play Store |
| Package Name | Unique identifier, e.g. `com.yourname.appname` |
| Version | Semantic version like `1.0.0` |
| App Icon | 1024×1024px PNG |

---

## Step 1: Generate an Android Build in Rork

From your Rork project dashboard, initiate an Android build:

```
1. Open your Rork project
2. Click "Deploy" in the top-right corner
3. Select "Android" as the platform
4. Enable "Build for Play Store"
5. Start the build (this may take a few minutes)
```

Once complete, download your **AAB (Android App Bundle)** file. Google Play requires AAB format rather than APK for all new submissions—it's more efficient and produces smaller app downloads for users.

> **Important**: Rork Max currently targets Apple platforms only (native Swift/SwiftUI). If you need to publish to Google Play, use the original Rork with React Native.

---

## Step 2: Create Your App in Google Play Console

1. Go to [Google Play Console](https://play.google.com/console) and sign in
2. Click **"Create app"**
3. Fill in the basic info:

```
App name: Your app's display name
Default language: English (or your primary market)
App or game: App
Free or paid: Your choice
```

4. Accept the developer policies and click **"Create app"**

---

## Step 3: Set Up Your Store Listing

Before submitting for review, complete your store listing. This is what users will see on the Play Store page.

### Required Metadata

```yaml
App name: Up to 50 characters
Short description: Up to 80 characters (appears in search results)
Full description: Up to 4,000 characters
```

### Required Assets

| Asset | Dimensions | Quantity |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| App icon | 512×512px PNG | 1 |
| Feature graphic | 1024×500px | 1 |
| Phone screenshots | 320–3840px (any ratio) | 2–8 |
| Tablet screenshots | Optional | — |

### Category Selection

Choose the category that best describes your app (e.g., Tools, Lifestyle, Productivity, Education). This affects discoverability in the Play Store browse experience.

---

## Step 4: Complete App Content Settings

Google requires you to declare the nature of your app's content and data practices.

### Content Rating

Navigate to **Policy → App content → Content rating** and complete the questionnaire. This generates an age-appropriate rating (similar to ESRB or PEGI for games) and is mandatory before submitting.

### Data Safety

Navigate to **Policy → App content → Data safety** and declare what data your app collects:

```
Examples:
- Does the app collect location? → No (unless your app uses GPS)
- Does it collect email addresses? → Yes (if you have login/auth)
- Is data encrypted in transit? → Yes (Expo apps use HTTPS by default)
```

Rork apps built with Firebase, Supabase, or similar backends will collect certain user data—check your integrations to make accurate declarations. Inaccurate data safety forms can result in removal.

---

## Step 5: Upload Your AAB and Choose a Release Track

Google Play supports multiple release tracks. Use them strategically:

| Track | Audience |
|-------|----------|
| Internal testing | Up to 100 testers (team members) |
| Closed testing (Alpha) | Invite-only group |
| Open testing (Beta) | Any user who opts in |
| Production | All Play Store users |

For a first release, we recommend: **Internal testing → Open testing → Production**.

### Uploading the AAB

```
1. Go to "Release → Production" (or Internal testing)
2. Click "Create new release"
3. Under "App bundles," click "Upload" and select your .aab file
4. Add release notes (e.g., "Initial release")
5. Click "Save → Review release → Rollout to production"
```

---

## Step 6: Submit for Review and Go Live

After submitting, Google's review team evaluates your app. Here's what to expect:

- **Review time**: Typically **1–3 business days** (can be up to 1 week for first-time submissions)
- **Notification**: Via email and Play Console dashboard
- **Rejection**: Address the flagged issues and resubmit

### Common Rejection Reasons

```
1. Screenshots don't match the actual app UI
2. Misleading or exaggerated claims in the description
3. Inaccurate Data Safety declarations
4. Broken links within the app
5. Missing or invalid Privacy Policy URL
```

> **Heads up**: A valid Privacy Policy URL is required for all apps. If your Rork app doesn't have one yet, host it on a web page and add the URL in Play Console before submitting.

## Rork-Specific Tips

### Package Name is Permanent

Once your app is published to the Play Store, the **package name cannot be changed**. Choose something like `com.yourname.appname` that you're happy with long-term. Set it in Rork before your first build.

### Version Code Must Increment

Every time you update your app, the `versionCode` (an integer) must be higher than the previous release. Check your Expo config:

```js
// app.json (Expo config)
{
  "expo": {
    "android": {
      "versionCode": 2  // Must be greater than previous upload
    }
  }
}
```

### 64-bit Support is Included

Google Play has required 64-bit support since 2019. Rork's Expo-based build system generates 64-bit compatible AABs by default—no extra configuration needed.

### Testing on a Real Device

Before submitting, test your AAB on a physical Android device using the [bundletool](https://developer.android.com/tools/bundletool) CLI or by sharing your Internal Testing track with a test device. Emulators can miss device-specific issues.

## Summary

Publishing a Rork app to Google Play comes down to six steps:

1. **Register** a Google Play Developer account ($25 one-time fee)
2. **Generate** an AAB build from your Rork project
3. **Create your app** in Play Console and fill in store listing info
4. **Declare** your content rating and data safety practices
5. **Upload** your AAB and choose the right release track
6. **Submit** for review and go live

Google Play's lower barrier to entry—a one-time $25 fee vs. Apple's $99/year—makes it a great starting point for indie developers. With Rork handling the cross-platform build, you can ship to both Android and iOS without writing platform-specific code.

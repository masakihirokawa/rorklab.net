## The Dream of Building Apps Without Programming

"I have a great idea, but I can't program."

Many people face this challenge. However, in 2026, **with AI power, anyone without programming experience can publish an app to the App Store**.

This article shows the complete workflow that makes this possible.

## Meet Our Example: Tanaka

**Tanaka: 35 years old, works in sales**
- No programming experience
- Has ideas for apps that would be useful
- Wants to actually publish an app to the App Store

Let's follow Tanaka's journey to building an app.

## Step 1: Market Research for Your Idea (Google AI Deep Search)

Tanaka's idea: "An app that records daily blood pressure and blood sugar data, with AI providing health advice"

First, we need to confirm: "Does this app already exist?" and "Is there a market?"

### Research with Deep Search

```
Tanaka opens Google AI Pro

Search keywords:
"Health management app blood pressure blood sugar AI 2026 market"

Deep Search automatically researches:
- 10 existing health management apps
- Each app's features and pricing
- User reviews
- Market size
- Regulatory requirements (medical app classification)
```

Results:

```
1. Similar apps do exist, but
   "Apps specifically focused on AI advice" are relatively rare

2. User needs indicate:
   "Medically reliable advice" is in high demand

3. Market size: 2-5 billion yen annually in Japan

4. Regulations: Medical information management is mandatory
   (Privacy policy, etc.)
```

Market research usually takes 3-4 hours, but Deep Search completes it in **just 15 minutes**.

## Step 2: Organizing App Specifications (NotebookLM)

Based on the research results, we'll finalize the app's detailed specifications.

### Upload to NotebookLM

```
Tanaka opens NotebookLM

Upload the following:
1. Deep Search research results
2. Introduction pages from 3 existing apps
3. Medical app regulatory requirements (Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare webpage)
4. Tanaka's hand-written "app idea"

Then ask the AI:
"Based on market research and regulatory requirements,
what are the essential features our app should have?"

NotebookLM's response:
1. Blood pressure and blood sugar input (daily)
2. Medically reliable AI advice
3. Privacy protection (strict medical information management)
4. Feature for sharing information with users' doctors
5. Data analysis (trend identification)
```

This clarifies "what should be built."

## Step 3: Create App Business Plan (Gemini)

Create a simple business plan based on NotebookLM's analysis.

### Instruct Gemini

```
"Based on the following information,
create a business plan for the app store:

Market research: [Paste NotebookLM output]
Essential features: [Above list]

The plan should include:
- App description (under 100 words)
- Introduction to main features
- Target users
- Price setting proposal
- Catchy tagline"

Example Gemini output:
---
# Health Advisor: AI Supports Your Daily Health

## Description
Record your blood pressure and blood sugar daily.
AI provides medically reliable advice.
Share information with your doctor if needed.

## Main Features
1. Easy input (completed in 2 minutes daily)
2. AI advice (with medical backing)
3. Trend analysis
4. Privacy protection

## Target
People aged 40-65 interested in health management

## Price
Free version: Basic features only
Premium: ¥500/month (Enhanced AI advice)

## Tagline
"Your health, protected by AI"
---
```

## Step 4: Build the App (Rork)

Now it's time to implement. This is where Rork comes in.

### Building with Rork

```
Access Rork
(Register and create an account first)

Create "New Project"

Project name: HealthAdvisor
Template selection: Health & Wellness

Rork suggests basic template:
- Home screen (data input form)
- Dashboard (data display)
- Settings screen (privacy settings)
```

### Creating Screens

Using Rork's UI builder, create these screens (drag-and-drop to completion):

**1. Home Screen**
- Blood pressure input field (systolic and diastolic)
- Blood sugar input field
- "Record" button
- Today's advice display area

**2. Dashboard**
- Graph display of past 30 days
- Average value display
- Trend (increasing/decreasing/stable)

**3. Privacy Settings**
- Medical information management policy
- Data deletion options

### Integrating AI Logic

```
Configure API integration in Rork

"Connect Google Gemini"

Flow:
User inputs data
  ↓
Send to Gemini
  ↓
Generate medical advice
  ↓
Display to user
```

Rork automatically generates this flow.

### Testing and Adjusting

```
Use Rork's "Preview" feature to
confirm actual operation on smartphone

If issues exist, fix in Rork
(No code editing needed, fix via UI builder)
```

## Step 5: Create Promotional Video (Google AI Veo 3)

App Store listings are more effective with a video.

### Generate Video with Veo 3

```
Open Veo 3 in Google AI Pro

Prompt:
"Create a promotional video for the HealthAdvisor app.

Content:
- App launching on smartphone
- User inputting blood pressure and blood sugar
- AI providing medical advice
- Data displaying as a graph
- Final message: "Your health, protected by AI"

Style:
- Professional
- Clean, medical app aesthetic
- Under 60 seconds"

Veo 3 generates a high-quality video (720p, ~60 seconds)
```

This video can be placed directly on your App Store listing.

## Step 6: Register and Launch on App Store

### Required Materials

```
1. App icon (Can be created from Veo-generated image)
2. Screenshots (3-5, exported from Rork)
3. Promotional video (Generated by Veo)
4. Description text (Generated by Gemini)
5. Privacy policy (Created from NotebookLM's medical information management notes)
```

### Registering in App Store Connect

```
Log in to App Store Connect

Create "New App"

Enter:
- App name: HealthAdvisor
- Description: Text created by Gemini
- Keywords: health, blood pressure, blood sugar, AI
- Screenshots: Export from Rork
- Preview video: Video generated by Veo

Finally, click "Submit for Review"
```

### Apple's Review Period

Typically 1-3 days for Apple's review to complete.

If no issues, the app automatically launches on the App Store.

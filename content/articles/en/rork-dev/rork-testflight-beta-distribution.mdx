## What Is TestFlight?

TestFlight is Apple's official beta testing platform for distributing pre-release versions of your app to a select group of testers. Before pushing your app to the App Store, TestFlight lets you gather real-world feedback, catch bugs, and refine the user experience.

With TestFlight, you can distribute your app to up to 10,000 external testers and 100 internal testers. Testers can submit feedback and screenshots directly from the app, and each beta build remains valid for 90 days. For developers using Rork Max, TestFlight fits naturally into the workflow thanks to the platform's built-in cloud compilation and deployment features.

## The End-to-End Workflow: Rork Max to TestFlight

Getting your Rork Max app onto TestFlight follows a straightforward pipeline. First, you finish building your app in Rork Max and trigger a release build through the cloud compiler. Next, you register your app in App Store Connect and upload the build. Finally, you invite testers through the TestFlight tab and start collecting feedback.

What makes this process especially smooth with Rork Max is the 2-click publish feature. Instead of juggling Xcode certificates and provisioning profiles manually, Rork Max handles most of the heavy lifting through its cloud Mac infrastructure. You can go from code to TestFlight distribution without ever opening Xcode on your local machine.

## Setting Up Your App in App Store Connect

Before you can use TestFlight, your app needs to be registered in App Store Connect. This requires an active Apple Developer Program membership ($99 per year).

Log in to App Store Connect and navigate to the "My Apps" section. Click the plus button to create a new app entry. You will need to provide the bundle ID that matches what Rork Max generated for your project — typically in the format `com.rork.yourappname`. Fill in the app name, primary language, and SKU, then select iOS as the platform.

Getting the bundle ID right is critical. If it does not match the one embedded in your Rork Max build, the upload will fail. Double-check the identifier in your Rork Max project settings before proceeding.

## Generating and Uploading Builds from Rork Max

Rork Max includes a cloud-based build system that eliminates the need for a local Xcode installation. When your app is ready for testing, navigate to your Rork Max dashboard and click the "Build" button. The platform spins up a cloud Mac instance, compiles your Swift code, and produces a signed IPA file.

Once the build finishes, Rork Max can automatically upload it to App Store Connect. This entire process typically takes just a few minutes, depending on your app's complexity. After the upload completes, the build will appear in the TestFlight tab of App Store Connect with a "Processing" status. Wait a few minutes for Apple's servers to finish processing — this is normal and usually completes within 15 to 30 minutes.

## Configuring Internal Testing

Internal testing targets members of your App Store Connect team — up to 100 people. The biggest advantage of internal testing is that it skips Apple's beta review process entirely, which means testers can start using your app within minutes of uploading a new build.

To set up an internal test group, go to the TestFlight tab in App Store Connect, find the "Internal Testing" section, and create a new group. Add team members by their email addresses. Each person will receive an invitation email with instructions to download the TestFlight app and install your beta.

Every time you upload a new build, internal testers are automatically notified. This makes internal testing ideal for rapid iteration during active development cycles.

## Setting Up External Testing and the Beta Review

External testing extends your reach to up to 10,000 testers outside your team. Unlike internal testing, your first external build requires a Beta App Review from Apple. This review is lighter than a full App Store review, but your app still needs to meet basic functionality and privacy requirements. Most beta reviews complete within 24 to 48 hours.

You have two ways to add external testers. You can invite them individually by email address, or you can generate a public link that anyone with the URL can use to join. Public links work well when you want to recruit testers through social media, forums, or blog posts. You can set a maximum number of testers for each public link to control distribution.

After the initial review, subsequent builds to the same test group usually do not require another review, as long as the changes are minor.

## Collecting and Acting on Tester Feedback

One of TestFlight's strongest features is structured feedback collection. Testers can take screenshots within the app and attach notes describing what they experienced. All feedback lands in the App Store Connect dashboard, where you can review it in a centralized feed.

Crash reports are also collected automatically. When a crash occurs, TestFlight records the stack trace, device model, OS version, and other diagnostic information. This data is invaluable for debugging in Rork Max — you can correlate crash patterns with specific device types or OS versions and prioritize fixes accordingly.

After reviewing feedback, go back to Rork Max, make the necessary changes, and upload a new build. For internal testers, the updated version becomes available immediately. For external testers, minor updates often skip the re-review process, keeping your iteration cycle fast.

## Common TestFlight Issues and How to Fix Them

A few problems come up regularly during beta testing, and knowing how to handle them saves time.

If your build upload fails, check that the bundle ID and provisioning profile match. Since Rork Max handles most certificate management through its cloud infrastructure, the most common cause is an expired or misconfigured Apple Developer account. Make sure your credentials are current.

When testers report they never received the invitation email, ask them to check their spam folder first. If the email still does not arrive, switch to sharing a public TestFlight link instead — this bypasses email delivery issues entirely.

Keep an eye on the 90-day expiration window. If your beta testing period stretches beyond three months, you will need to upload a fresh build to keep testers active. Plan your testing timeline accordingly and communicate deadlines to your testers.

## Moving from TestFlight to the App Store

Once you have gathered enough feedback and feel confident about your app's quality, transitioning to a full App Store release is seamless. The build you validated through TestFlight can be submitted directly for App Store review — there is no need to compile anything new.

In App Store Connect, switch to the "App Store" tab and select the same build that your testers have been using. Fill in the required metadata including screenshots, descriptions, keywords, and promotional text. Then submit for review.

Rork Max's 2-click publish feature streamlines this final step even further. Combined with the insights you gained during beta testing, you can submit a polished, thoroughly tested app with confidence.

## Wrapping Up

TestFlight and Rork Max together create a powerful beta testing workflow that is accessible even to solo developers. Cloud compilation removes the barrier of needing a Mac with Xcode, and TestFlight's built-in feedback tools give you direct access to what real users think of your app.

Start with a small group of internal testers to iron out the biggest issues, then expand to external testers for broader validation. The feedback you collect during this phase directly translates into better App Store reviews and higher user satisfaction at launch. TestFlight is free, it integrates natively with your existing Apple Developer tools, and it can make the difference between a rough launch and a smooth one.

## What Is TestFlight?

TestFlight is Apple's official beta testing platform. It lets you distribute pre-release builds of your app to a limited group of testers before you publish on the App Store.

Because Rork Max compiles native Swift apps in the cloud, you can generate an IPA file and upload it straight to TestFlight — no local Xcode installation required.

## Why Beta Testing Matters

Shipping an app without beta testing is like publishing a book without proofreading. Real users on real devices will uncover crashes, layout issues, and usability problems that never surface during development.

TestFlight offers two tiers. Internal testing delivers builds instantly to up to 100 App Store Connect team members with no Apple review. External testing supports up to 10,000 testers, though the first build requires a lightweight beta app review from Apple.

## Prerequisites

Before you can distribute through TestFlight, you need a few things in place.

An **Apple Developer Program** membership is required. It costs $99 per year and is available to both individuals and organizations.

A **Rork Max subscription** is needed to access cloud compilation and generate native Swift builds.

Your app must be **registered in App Store Connect** with a Bundle ID and basic metadata filled in.

## Generating a Build from Rork Max

Once your app has reached a testable state, it's time to create a build.

Check the version number and build number in your Rork Max project settings. Every upload to TestFlight requires a unique build number, so keep them incrementing — for example, `1.0.0 (1)`, `1.0.0 (2)`, and so on.

Hit the "Build" button in the Rork Max editor. The cloud Mac compiles your Swift code and produces an IPA file, typically in a few minutes. Download the IPA once the build succeeds.

```
Rork Max Editor
  → Build button
  → Cloud compilation starts
  → IPA file generated (a few minutes)
  → Download
```

If the build fails, try the "Fix Now" feature. It often resolves dependency issues and minor code errors automatically.

## Setting Up Your App in App Store Connect

Sign in to [App Store Connect](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com) and navigate to "My Apps." Select your app, or create a new one if you haven't already.

Fill in the essentials: app name, primary language, Bundle ID, and SKU. The Bundle ID must match what you configured in your Rork Max project.

## Uploading the Build to TestFlight

There are a couple of ways to get your IPA into TestFlight.

The **Transporter app** (free on the Mac App Store) is the easiest option. Drag and drop your IPA file, and Transporter handles the rest.

For automation, the **altool command line** works well, especially if you're building a CI/CD pipeline.

```bash
xcrun altool --upload-app \
  --type ios \
  --file YourApp.ipa \
  --apiKey YOUR_API_KEY \
  --apiIssuer YOUR_ISSUER_ID
```

After the upload completes, Apple runs automated processing — binary validation, encryption checks, and so on. This usually takes 5 to 15 minutes, after which the build appears in the TestFlight tab of App Store Connect.

## Configuring Internal Testing

Internal testing is the fastest way to get your app into testers' hands.

Open the TestFlight tab in App Store Connect and create a new group under "Internal Testing." Give it a descriptive name like "Core Team" or "Dev Squad."

To add testers, they must be registered as users in your App Store Connect account. Enter their Apple ID email addresses to send invitations. Testers receive an email and can install the beta through the TestFlight app on their iPhone or iPad.

Because internal builds skip Apple's review process, they're available to testers immediately after upload.

## Configuring External Testing

When you're ready for broader feedback, set up external testing.

Create a new group under "External Testing" and add testers by email address. External testers don't need to be App Store Connect users — any email address works.

You'll need to provide some test information.

**What to test**: Give testers clear instructions. "Please try the new checkout flow and report any issues" is far more useful than "Test the app."

**Feedback email**: Set the email address where tester reports will land.

**Privacy policy URL**: Required for external testing. Link to a page that explains what data your app collects.

The first external build goes through a beta app review, which typically takes 24 to 48 hours. Subsequent builds to the same external group usually don't require another review.

## Using Public Links to Recruit Testers

External testing also supports public invite links. Share the link on social media or your website, and anyone can join without you manually entering their email.

You can cap the number of testers on a public link. A practical approach is to start with 100 slots, monitor how feedback comes in, and increase the limit as you're ready to handle more.

## Collecting and Acting on Feedback

One of TestFlight's strongest features is structured feedback collection.

Testers can send feedback directly from the TestFlight app, attaching screenshots and written notes. All submissions appear in the TestFlight section of App Store Connect.

Crash reports are collected automatically. When a tester's device encounters a crash, the log is uploaded to App Store Connect. Analyzing these logs helps you pinpoint issues specific to certain devices or OS versions.

The workflow becomes a loop: collect feedback, fix issues in Rork Max, generate a new build, upload to TestFlight, and repeat. Each cycle raises the quality bar before your public launch.

## Best Practices for Effective Beta Testing

A few habits make beta testing significantly more productive.

**Write a test plan.** Decide what you're testing and on which devices before you ship the build. Focusing on specific areas per release — rather than testing everything at once — keeps feedback actionable.

**Cover a range of devices.** From iPhone SE to iPad Pro, screen sizes vary dramatically. Both Rork's React Native apps and Rork Max's native Swift apps can behave differently across form factors.

**Communicate with your testers.** Let them know what to focus on, when new builds drop, and which reported issues have been fixed. Testers who feel heard stay engaged.

**Roll out in stages.** Start with internal testing (5–10 people) to catch showstoppers, then expand to external testing (50–100 people) for broader feedback. This two-phase approach keeps things manageable.

## From TestFlight to the App Store

Once beta testing confirms your app is ready, you can submit the same build to the App Store. Any build uploaded to TestFlight is selectable in the App Store release workflow within App Store Connect.

Combined with Rork Max's 2-click App Store publishing feature, the path from TestFlight validation to a live App Store listing is remarkably smooth.

## Wrapping Up

Rork Max and TestFlight together give you a professional beta testing pipeline without requiring a Mac development environment. Generate builds in the cloud, distribute through TestFlight, gather feedback, iterate — and ship with confidence.

Even solo developers benefit enormously from handing a beta to just a handful of friends or family. The bugs they find in five minutes of casual use can save you from one-star reviews on launch day. That small investment of time before release makes all the difference.

## Introduction — Why Your Commission Rate Matters More Than You Think

Building an app with Rork is only half the journey. Once your app is live on the App Store or Google Play, the platform takes a cut of every sale. At the standard 30% commission rate, a month with $1,000 in revenue means $300 goes straight to Apple before you see a cent.

But there's a program that cuts that in half — and most indie developers haven't enrolled yet.

Apple's **App Store Small Business Program** reduces the standard 30% commission to **15%** for developers who earn under $1 million USD per year. For a developer earning $1,000/month, that's an extra $150 in take-home pay every single month, or $1,800 per year — just from a 10-minute enrollment.

This guide walks you through everything Rork developers need to know: eligibility requirements, the enrollment process, Google Play's equivalent program, and how to calculate your actual revenue impact.

---

## What Is the App Store Small Business Program?

Launched in November 2020, the App Store Small Business Program is Apple's initiative to support independent developers and small studios. If your prior calendar year's net proceeds from the App Store were under **$1 million USD**, you qualify for a reduced 15% commission on all sales.

### What Sales Are Covered?

The reduced rate applies to:

- **Paid app downloads** (upfront purchase price)
- **In-app purchases (IAP)** — consumable, non-consumable, and non-renewing subscriptions
- **Auto-renewable subscriptions** — at 15% from day one (see below)

Note that advertising revenue from third-party networks like AdMob is not processed through Apple, so it falls outside this program.

### The Subscription Advantage

Normally, Apple's standard commission on auto-renewable subscriptions drops from 30% to 15% after a subscriber maintains the same subscription for **one year**. When you're enrolled in the Small Business Program, this one-year waiting period is eliminated. You receive **15% from the first month** on all subscriptions.

If you're building a subscription app with Rork — which is increasingly the model for sustainable indie app revenue — this benefit alone can make a significant difference in your early months of growth.

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the Small Business Program, you need to meet all of the following:

**Core requirements:**
- Active enrollment in the Apple Developer Program (individual or organization)
- Prior calendar year net proceeds from the App Store across **all your accounts** of less than $1 million USD
- All Apple Developer Program accounts you own, manage, or belong to must collectively fall under the $1 million threshold

**Key notes:**
- If you have multiple developer accounts, Apple counts the combined revenue across all of them
- Brand-new developers with no prior sales history are automatically eligible
- If you exceed $1 million in a given year, you'll return to the standard 30% rate starting the following calendar year

For the vast majority of Rork developers just starting out or in the early stages of monetization, you almost certainly qualify. The bigger risk is *not* enrolling and paying an extra 15% when you don't have to.

## Step-by-Step Enrollment Process

Enrolling in the Small Business Program takes about 5 to 10 minutes and is done entirely through App Store Connect.

### Step 1: Sign In to App Store Connect

Go to [appstoreconnect.apple.com](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com) and sign in with your Apple ID.

### Step 2: Open Agreements, Tax, and Banking

From the App Store Connect homepage, navigate to **Agreements, Tax, and Banking** (or "契約/税金/口座情報" if your interface is in Japanese).

### Step 3: Find the Small Business Program Section

Within this section, you'll see a dedicated area for the **App Store Small Business Program**. If you're eligible, an **"Enroll"** button will be visible.

### Step 4: Complete the Enrollment Form

Follow the on-screen prompts to provide information about your developer status — whether you're an individual or organization, and whether you have any affiliated accounts. The form is straightforward.

### Step 5: Wait for Approval

Apple typically reviews and approves applications within a few business days. You'll receive an email notification when your enrollment is confirmed. Once approved, the new 15% rate applies to sales in the following calendar month.

```
Tip: After approval, return to the Agreements, Tax, and Banking section
to confirm your Small Business Program status shows as "Active."
```

## Google Play's Equivalent Program

If you're shipping your Rork app on Android as well as iOS — which is straightforward with Rork's cross-platform approach — Google Play also offers a reduced commission structure.

**Google Play Service Fee Program:**

- The first **$1 million USD** in annual revenue is subject to a **15% service fee**
- Revenue above $1 million USD reverts to the standard 30% fee
- In most cases, this is applied automatically based on your account history — no separate application required

One practical difference from Apple's program: Google's structure means that even if you surpass $1 million in a year, only the portion above that threshold is taxed at 30%. Apple's program applies the full 30% rate once you exceed the threshold in a prior year.

## Calculating Your Real Revenue Impact

Let's put some concrete numbers on what enrollment is actually worth.

**Scenario: A subscription app priced at $4.99/month**

- 100 active subscribers
- Monthly revenue: $499

Before enrollment (30% fee): Commission $149.70 / Take-home $349.30
After enrollment (15% fee): Commission $74.85 / Take-home $424.15 / Annual difference +$900/year

```
Annual take-home increase: $74.85 × 12 months = $898.20
```

Scale this to 1,000 subscribers and you're looking at nearly $9,000 more per year from a single enrollment. The enrollment itself takes less time than making a coffee.

For developers building subscription-based apps with Rork, the combination of this program with a well-designed pricing model can compound significantly. ## Things Rork Developers Should Keep in Mind

A few practical considerations specific to Rork's ecosystem and workflow:

**Plan your IAP structure during development**

Rork and Rork Max support integration with RevenueCat and StoreKit 2 for handling subscriptions and in-app purchases. When you're designing your app's monetization model — whether it's a one-time purchase, subscription, or freemium with upgrades — factor the 15% rate into your pricing calculations from the start. It changes the math on what price point makes sense.

**Annual renewal and monitoring**

The Small Business Program renews automatically each calendar year, but Apple reassesses your eligibility based on prior year revenue. If you're growing quickly, set a reminder to check your status in December each year. It's a good opportunity to review your pricing and monetization strategy as well.

**Company formation and account changes**

If you transition from a sole proprietor to a formal business entity, you may need to update your Apple Developer Program account and re-enroll in the Small Business Program under the new entity. Doing this before a significant launch can save administrative headaches.

**Verify your status before each major launch**

Before releasing a new app or a major update with new IAP, confirm that your Small Business Program status is still Active in App Store Connect. It's a quick check that's easy to skip but important to confirm.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I'm a brand-new developer with zero sales. Can I still enroll?**

Yes, absolutely. New developers with no prior App Store sales history are automatically eligible. The enrollment button should appear in your App Store Connect account. Enrolling before your first app launch means you benefit from the 15% rate from day one.

**Q: I only have free apps right now. Should I still enroll?**

There's no immediate financial benefit if your apps are entirely free. However, if you ever plan to add paid features, a premium tier, or in-app purchases, enrolling in advance means those features will be covered by the lower rate as soon as you add them. Enrollment is free and has no downsides, so it's worth doing now.

**Q: What happens if my revenue crosses $1 million USD?**

Your current year's sales continue at 15% through the end of that calendar year. Starting the following calendar year, the standard 30% rate applies. Apple will notify you in advance. Reaching this threshold is a great problem to have — and at that revenue level, you'll likely have resources to optimize your tax and business structure accordingly.

**Q: How does Apple calculate the $1 million threshold for Japanese developers selling in JPY?**

Apple converts App Store proceeds to USD using its own exchange rates for the purpose of this calculation. You can view your cumulative USD proceeds in the **Payments and Financial Reports** section of App Store Connect. With prolonged yen weakness, the USD equivalent of your yen revenue may be lower, which can actually work in your favor for staying under the threshold.

**Q: Can I enroll even if I use a third-party payment system alongside the App Store?**

The Small Business Program applies specifically to transactions processed through Apple's payment system. If you use alternative payment methods (where applicable under platform rules), those transactions are handled separately and are not subject to App Store commission.

## Summary

The App Store Small Business Program is one of the most straightforward ways to increase your take-home revenue as an indie developer — and yet it's still overlooked by many who would qualify.

Here's what to remember:

- Developers with prior-year App Store proceeds under $1 million USD qualify for 15% commission (down from 30%)
- Enrollment takes about 5 to 10 minutes via App Store Connect
- New developers with no prior sales history are automatically eligible
- Auto-renewable subscriptions get the 15% rate from month one (not after year one)
- Google Play has an equivalent 15% rate on the first $1 million in annual revenue, often applied automatically

When you're building with Rork and starting to think seriously about monetization, enrollment in this program should be one of your first steps. The application is free, approval is usually swift, and the ongoing benefit compounds with every sale you make.

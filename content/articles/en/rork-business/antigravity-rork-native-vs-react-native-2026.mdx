## The Fundamental Question: Which Framework Should You Choose?

Every app developer faces this question at some point: "Should I build with React Native (cross-platform) or SwiftUI (native)?"

In 2026, with AI tools transforming the landscape, **the answer is no longer straightforward**. The traditional trade-off between performance and development speed has shifted dramatically. Both approaches now allow for rapid development, making the decision less about technical capability and more about business strategy.

This article examines both paths in depth, helping you make an informed choice based on your project's actual requirements.

## Two Development Approaches

### 1. Antigravity + React Native

**Approach**: Use Antigravity to generate React Native code and manage cross-platform development.

**Advantages**:
- Develop iOS and Android simultaneously
- Share 70-80% of codebase across platforms
- Easy expansion to web with React
- Faster MVP development and market validation

**Disadvantages**:
- Limited access to native platform features in some cases
- Performance slightly lower than native implementations
- App Store review can be stricter for cross-platform apps

**Monthly Cost**: $49 (Antigravity subscription)

### 2. Rork Max + SwiftUI (Native)

**Approach**: Use Rork Max to build native iOS apps with SwiftUI, leveraging Apple's latest technologies.

**Advantages**:
- Full access to all iOS features and APIs
- Superior performance and battery efficiency
- Higher App Store approval rates for native apps
- Better optimization for Apple's latest devices

**Disadvantages**:
- iOS only (Android requires separate development)
- Longer initial development cycle than React Native
- Requires understanding of iOS-specific patterns

**Monthly Cost**: $99 (Rork Max subscription)

## Final Recommendation Framework

The "React Native vs. SwiftUI" question doesn't have a one-size-fits-all answer. Instead, ask yourself:

1. **What is my target platform?** (iOS only vs. iOS + Android)
2. **What are my performance requirements?** (luxury app vs. utility app)
3. **What's my priority?** (speed to market vs. quality vs. cost)
4. **What's my long-term vision?** (single app vs. app ecosystem)

Once you've answered these questions clearly, the optimal technology choice becomes obvious.

In 2026, with AI accelerating both approaches, the question isn't "which technology is better" but rather "which aligns with my business strategy?" The right answer depends entirely on your goals.

Choose wisely, and build something great.

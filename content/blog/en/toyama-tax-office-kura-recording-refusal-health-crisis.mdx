## Background

For the full context of this ongoing situation, please see:

- [Fighting Injustice with AI Tax Filing](/blog/ai-tax-filing-and-fighting-injustice)

In brief: I am a sole proprietor running an export business (app development with ad revenue from overseas corporations). Under Article 7 of Japan's Consumption Tax Act, my business income should have been tax-exempt. Despite this, I overpaid approximately 5 million yen in consumption tax over the years. When I requested a refund from Miyazaki Tax Office in 2021, they refused and would not even let me file an amended return. Instead, they offered a "tax-exempt filing" arrangement until 2024 — effectively running out the statute of limitations, then reversing course and demanding payment for the tax-exempt period.

## April 15, 2026 — Phone Call with Mr. Kura of Toyama Tax Office

On April 15th, I called Mr. Kura at the Toyama Tax Office. Given his history of intimidating behavior, I informed him that I would be recording the call.

His response: "I can't talk if you're recording."

That single statement says everything.

A public servant carrying out official duties refuses to have his words documented. The only reasonable interpretation is that he knows his conduct would not withstand scrutiny if recorded.

Refusing dialogue simply because a call is being recorded effectively denies a taxpayer the right to protect themselves with evidence. I have every legitimate reason to document these interactions.

## Continued Refusal to Return Overpaid Taxes

Mr. Kura continues to maintain his stance of refusing any refund of the overpaid consumption tax.

They will not return the approximately 5 million yen that should never have been collected. They blocked the amended return process. They let the statute of limitations expire. They then demanded payment for the period they themselves classified as tax-exempt. And when questioned about all of this, the response is: "I can't talk if you're recording."

This is the reality of tax administration in Japan.

## Worsening Health Crisis

This pattern of behavior has caused my gastric ulcer to relapse.

Severe abdominal pain and nausea persist. I can barely eat or sleep. On April 16, 2026, my condition deteriorated to the point where I had to call an ambulance.

Since November 2021, I have been battling adjustment disorder, depression, insomnia, gastric ulcers, and reflux esophagitis — all stemming from the stress of dealing with tax authorities. For over five years, I have barely been able to maintain my apps, let alone create new work. My career as an artist has been completely frozen.

My monthly income after expenses is approximately 45,000 yen, yet my monthly tax repayments exceed 60,000 yen. I continue to pay more than I earn while also supporting four children.

## Next Steps

I have reached my limit — but not in the sense of giving up.

I am proceeding with a formal legal consultation through Legal Aid Japan (Houterasu) and filing an official complaint with the Nagoya Regional Taxation Bureau.

I have prepared a comprehensive report (Version 3) documenting everything, including Mr. Kura's recording refusal, intimidating behavior, and the resulting health emergency.

I am keeping this record not just for myself, but for anyone who has faced similar injustice from those in power. I refuse to suffer in silence. That is the one thing I can still do.

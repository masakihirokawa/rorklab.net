## An App Developer's Battle with Wrongful Taxation

I develop iPhone and Android apps independently, with AdMob advertising as my primary revenue source. Under Japan's Consumption Tax Act (Articles 7 and 17), AdMob revenue constitutes a service provided to an overseas entity (Google LLC, a US-based company) and is exempt from consumption tax.

Yet I was charged approximately ¥5 million ($33,000 USD) in consumption tax that I never legally owed — partly because my tax accountant at the time did not understand that digital advertising revenue qualifies as an export service. My 2021 refund request was denied by the Miyazaki Tax Office. By 2024, the Uozu Tax Office claimed the statute of limitations had expired and demanded I repay the tax-exempt treatment from the interim period.

My monthly income is approximately ¥45,000 ($300 USD). The demanded monthly repayment is ¥60,668 ($400 USD). For an indie app developer, this is effectively a financial death sentence.

## Local Offices Review the Case — Uozu Tax Office Refuses

The Nyuzen Town Office and Namerikawa City Tax Division responded that my case is "under review" — a constructive sign. The Uozu Tax Office, however, maintained its refusal.

Officer Mr. Y refused to meet in person, speaking only through a counter window. After I explained the legal basis for my claim, he repeated the office's position without engaging with the substance. When I told him the conversation was being recorded, he immediately ended it: "I cannot continue this discussion any further."

When a public servant fears being recorded during official duties, it tells you everything about whether their conduct can withstand scrutiny. Uozu Tax Office is complicit in perpetuating wrongful taxation.

## What Every App Developer Needs to Know About Consumption Tax

This is directly relevant to Rork Lab's readers, many of whom are app developers.

Revenue from AdMob, Google AdSense, and overseas in-app purchases is exempt from Japan's consumption tax. The legal basis under Articles 7 and 17 of the Consumption Tax Act is clear and unambiguous — these constitute services provided to overseas entities.

Here is the critical problem: **most Japanese tax accountants do not know this.** The majority classify digital advertising revenue as ordinary domestic business income subject to consumption tax. Tax office staff make the same error. Physical goods exports are easily recognized as tax-exempt, but digital service exports remain a systemic blind spot.

This means that even if you hire a tax accountant, your return may be filed incorrectly. If you, as an app developer, do not understand your own revenue's tax classification, no one will protect you.

I'm Masaki Hirokawa — an artist and creator.

I've been developing iOS and Android apps as an indie developer since 2014. My apps — primarily in the wallpaper, healing, and manifestation genres — have collectively surpassed 50 million downloads. At peak, AdMob alone was bringing in about ¥1.5 million (around $10,000) per month, remitted from overseas, which qualified as export-exempt revenue under Article 7 of Japan's Consumption Tax Act.

At the same time, I'm an artist holding 17 international design awards and ranked the World's 14th Best Designer at the A' Design Award DAC (2025-2026). I've exhibited across Europe, Asia, and the Americas.

That life — built on two pillars of app development and art — began to fracture in 2022, when the Toyama and Uozu Tax Offices retroactively overturned years of correctly filed export-exemption returns. Over the following four years, I developed adjustment disorder, depression, insomnia, and a recurring gastric ulcer. By 2025, I had filed for personal bankruptcy, gone through divorce, and been separated from my four children. On April 16, 2026, I was rushed to the emergency room during a phone call with a tax officer. Two days later, on April 20, I walk into a lawyer's office for the first time.

This page exists for other indie developers who earn their revenue through overseas platforms — AdMob, Google Play, App Store, Stripe, Patreon, and the like. If you're reading this while afraid of a call from your local tax office about whether your export exemption will "really" be accepted, please let my records help you prepare.

## How It Began

In 2022, the Miyazaki Tax Office — which covered my residence at the time — called to say my "overpaid consumption tax case" would be transferred to the Uozu Tax Office (Toyama Regional Taxation Bureau). AdMob pays in foreign currency from overseas, so I had always filed the revenue as export-exempt and received the corresponding refunds, year after year.

What arrived after that transfer, however, was **retroactive taxation timed precisely to the statute of limitations under Article 74 of Japan's General Act of National Taxes**. Past export exemptions were declared invalid and I was ordered to pay back more than ¥5 million, while my own refund claims were rejected as "time-barred."

The savings I had built up to keep my app development business running, and the life of my family, unraveled in the wake of a single bureaucratic maneuver.

## Why I'm Publishing This

App development is a lonely profession. When a tax officer calls, you don't always have a trusted accountant nearby. Export-exemption cases for overseas platform revenue are rarely familiar territory even for local tax officials — and in that knowledge gap, a single officer's interpretation can move millions of yen of liability.

To fellow developers earning via AdMob, Google Play, App Store, Stripe, Patreon, or overseas SaaS billing: I want you to know this kind of retroactive taxation can happen, and that with the right records and preparation, it is possible to fight back.

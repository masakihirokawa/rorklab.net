I've built iPhone and Android apps as a solo developer for years. For a stretch, AdMob revenue paid the bills — and I spent the whole time remitting consumption tax I didn't owe. By the time I understood that roughly ¥5 million had been overpaid, the statute of limitations on correction claims had closed, year by year. Today I'm being asked to pay back more than ¥5 million in addition, on top of never recovering the overpayment. I've been living with adjustment disorder, depression, insomnia, gastric ulcers, and reflux for five years, and I was recently taken to the emergency room.

I doubt I'm the only developer caught in this shape. This article is a practical guide for indie app developers earning from overseas ad platforms like AdMob: the export-exemption framework under Japan's consumption tax, and how to use AI to keep records and prepare the defense while things are still calm.

## The short answer: most AdMob revenue is export-exempt or non-taxable

In Japan, AdMob payouts to individual developers typically come from Google Asia Pacific Pte. Ltd. (Singapore) or Google Ireland Ltd. Providing ad inventory inside an app to an overseas entity is the kind of transaction covered by Article 7 of Japan's Consumption Tax Act (export exemption) and the "export-like transactions" of Article 17 of the Cabinet Order. Booking that revenue as domestic taxable sales means paying consumption tax you didn't owe.

The catch: applying the exemption requires conditions, and you need to demonstrate the facts of the transaction (counterparty, place of supply, currency, remittance path) with records. AI is extremely helpful at keeping those records and drafting the determination — but final judgment must stay with your tax accountant, legal aid, or the authorities themselves.

## Defense 1: Preserve AdMob contracts and payment history in order

At minimum, collect:

- AdMob / Google Play Console terms and contracts (with version and date)
- Monthly revenue statements and payout statements
- Bank deposit history (remitter name, currency)
- Country determination for your payment route (Singapore, Ireland, etc.)

My own records were fragmented, and that hurt me during the correction-claim discussions. If you're starting now, point an AI at your Drive folder for "AdMob / Payouts" and ask:

```
For each file in this folder, fill a Markdown table per fiscal year with:
- Contract version and effective period
- Monthly payout total, currency, remitter entity
- Quotations showing the payment originates from an overseas entity
- Missing evidence (notes)
```

The AI will read across scattered PDFs and statements and surface deltas — e.g., "payouts originate in Singapore for these years, but the remitter changed in that one."

## Defense 2: Re-classify the ledger's consumption-tax column

Before each filing, have the AI re-classify the consumption-tax column on your ledger. This is the fastest way to catch overpayment early.

```
This ledger is my full-year transactions as a solo founder. Using
counterparty country, currency, and service nature, re-classify
each row against Article 7 of the Consumption Tax Act and Article 17
of its Cabinet Order. State the reasoning and the additional
evidence needed to lock in each row's classification.
```

Years of overpayment frequently surface this way. If you find it, move quickly — correction claims are capped at five years from the statutory filing deadline. If you see any signs of a tax office refusing the claim mechanism or reframing it as "just treat it as non-taxable going forward," record everything in writing that day.

## Defense 3: Pre-draft Q&A for tax-office inquiries

Questions asked of app developers about AdMob exemption are more patterned than you'd expect. Ask your AI: "List 20 questions the tax office might ask when I claim AdMob revenue as export-exempt, and draft fact-based answers for each." Keep these on file; they accelerate any later review with your tax accountant or lawyer.

Be sure to cover:

- Place-of-supply determination (where are the ad servers, who are the advertisers)
- Match between invoicing country and contracting party
- Currency and remittance route
- Retention period for evidence (seven years statutory, ten years is safer in practice)
- Responding to private-life questions from tax officers (organize which questions you have no duty to answer)

## Defense 4: First-hour playbook if you're challenged

If you hit "your export-exemption claim is not accepted" or "we won't return the overpayment," these first moves protect you:

- Same-day: write down the call or meeting with time, officer, and statements
- Announce recording to the other party; if refused, log the refusal itself
- Send an email follow-up titled "Confirmation of today's call"
- From your ledger, confirm which fiscal years remain within the five-year correction-claim window
- Know in advance who you can contact fastest: small-business associations, legal aid, tax accountants, chambers of commerce

AI handles the drafting, summarization, and cross-checking. It does not, however, accept judgment liability. Do not try to handle authorities AI-only.

## Defense 5: Protect your health

I spent five years on this dispute, stopped my art practice entirely, went through a divorce, and ended up in the ER. For solo app developers, your health is the biggest asset on the balance sheet. My own rules, hard-earned:

- Cap tax-work at 90 minutes a day, timed
- On unwell days, don't even ask the AI to "continue." Close the draft
- Any ER visit, prescription, or injection gets recorded in the case file
- If a tax-office call triggers chest pain or tachycardia, end the call and contact a physician

Indie development already concentrates a lot of responsibility in one person. Drawing clear boundaries around your body and mind belongs right next to the AI work, not after it.

If you're reading this, you might be a developer who's been paying consumption tax on overseas ad-platform revenue without questioning the default. The smallest first step today: gather your AdMob payout statements and bank deposit history into one folder, and ask an AI whether each payment is worth evaluating for export exemption. From there, organize judgment, preserve evidence, and — if the clock hasn't fully run — begin a correction claim.
